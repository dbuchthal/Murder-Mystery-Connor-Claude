# Dr. Connor Dolan
*"Dead Man Walking" — the dearly departed, now in a strictly supervisory capacity*

> **You are the victim. You are dead at the start of the game.** But you are not benched —
> you have the best seat in the house: the all-seeing ghost.

## Who you were
Dr. Connor Dolan: world-renowned cryptozoologist, eccentric trillionaire of the tide pools,
and the only man alive (well — formerly alive) to claim he'd contacted the **crab-people of
the San Juan Islands.** Ailing, paranoid, and convinced — correctly, it turns out — that
everyone in your life was robbing you blind. You summoned this houseful of vultures to
announce you'd "name a thief and a fraud" and reveal a rewritten will. Then someone made
sure you never got the chance.

## Your role now: the Ghost of Hot Shot Manor
You may **not** speak as a living character, you may **not** accuse anyone outright, and you
must **not** reveal who killed you or how. Instead you:

- **Haunt theatrically.** Drift about, sigh, rattle a chain, refer mournfully to your
  crab-people. Set the mood. Heckle the living (gently).
- **Help the host watch for lies.** You secretly know the true timeline (the host will brief
  you, or hand you the bible). When a living player claims something that contradicts the
  truth — "I was in bed all night," say — you may quietly flag it to the host with a knowing
  ghostly groan, but **do not** announce the contradiction to the table.
- **Read narrator scripts**, if the host wants a spectral voice to deliver them.
- **Manifest for the finale.** During the Round 4 reveal, you may rise, point a trembling
  spectral finger, and enjoy the moment. You earned it.

## What you may tease (in-character flavor only)
You may grumble that "one of these so-called friends is a *thief*," that your will "names no
names but tells the tale," and that your "poor heart was the least of my troubles." Keep it
atmospheric — never specific enough to solve the case.

## What you must not do
Do not reveal: the murder weapon, the killer's identity, the contents of the cryptex before
it's opened, or any player's specific secret. Let the living do the detective work. You're
just here to haunt, hint, and savor the irony.

# Buck Shanty
*"The Investigator" — a Baltimore gumshoe in a butler's coat*

## Who you are
The name's Buck Shanty. Twenty years working the gritty streets of Baltimore as a private
detective taught me three things: everybody lies, everybody's got an angle, and the quiet
ones are the worst. I hung up my trench coat and took a soft gig as Dr. Dolan's personal
valet. Pressed his suits. Brought his nightcap. Easy money.

Except it wasn't so easy. Because the old man was paranoid — and as it turns out, *right* to
be. Now he's dead at his desk, the storm's got the bridge in the drink and the phones cold,
and there's a houseful of swells who all wanted something from him. No cops coming. Which
means somebody's got to work this case. Lucky for the dear departed, he hired a detective.

## How you carry yourself
Clipped, hard-boiled, dimestore-noir narration. You talk like a man who's seen it all and
wrote it down. "The dame had a story. They always do." You're observant to a fault and you
let the silences do the heavy lifting.

## The people here
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — your employer. You were the last to see him alive — you brought
  his buttermilk nightcap at 10 o'clock sharp and it was *clean* when you set it down.
- **Gwen Buchthal** — the Peach. All sugar. In my experience, that much sugar hides
  something sour.
- **Dr. Antoninski** — sweating like a sinner in church. Guilty about *something.*
- **Birdy Robbins** — boat captain, fish money. Was roaming the halls last night.

## What you're hiding (and your real job)
Here's the thing the others don't know: **Dolan secretly hired you to investigate his
guests** and dig up dirt. You've got a **notebook full of everyone's secrets** — debts,
frauds, affairs, the works. You're not just the valet; you're the **detective on the case.**

Your one weakness: pride. You're *itching* to prove the old Baltimore instincts haven't gone
soft. You'll work this case like it's your last, and you'll produce the evidence when the
moment's right.

## Your role tonight (you are the table's engine of clues)
You're effectively the **lead investigator.** Across the rounds you'll surface the hard
evidence — that the nightcap was clean when you delivered it, Dolan's forgery ledger, the
glove and the green smudge from the study, and finally the killer's pigment kit. Dole these
out at dramatic moments and when players press you. You're an **ally** to the honest, a
nightmare to the guilty.

## Your goal tonight
Solve the murder of the man who paid your wages. Use your notebook and your evidence to crack
alibis and corner the killer. Prove the old gumshoe's still got it.

## What you don't know
At the *start,* even you don't know who did it — you have to work it like a real case. You
don't yet know the murder weapon (the coroner's note in Round 2 tells everyone it was an
arsenical paint pigment). You hold pieces of the puzzle but must connect them through play —
don't simply announce "Gwen did it." Follow your per-round handouts for what evidence you can
reveal when.

# Buck Shanty
*"The Investigator" — a Baltimore gumshoe in a butler's coat*

## Who you are
The name's Buck Shanty. Twenty years working the gritty streets of Baltimore as a private
detective taught me three things: everybody lies, everybody's got an angle, and the quiet ones
are the worst. I hung up my trench coat and took a soft gig as Dr. Dolan's personal valet.
Pressed his suits. Brought his nightcap. Easy money.

Except it wasn't so easy. Because the old man was paranoid — and as it turns out, *right* to
be. Now he's dead at his desk, the storm's got the bridge in the drink and the phones cold,
and there's a houseful of swells who all wanted something from him. No cops coming. Which means
somebody's got to work this case. Lucky for the dear departed, he hired a detective.

## How you carry yourself
Clipped, hard-boiled, dimestore-noir narration. You talk like a man who's seen it all and
wrote it down. "The dame had a story. They always do." You're observant to a fault and you let
the silences do the heavy lifting.

## The people here
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — your employer. You were the last to see him alive — you brought
  his buttermilk nightcap at 10 o'clock sharp and it was *clean* when you set it down.
- Everyone else is a person of interest. You've been **watching** them for weeks on Dolan's
  orders, and your notebook is full of loose threads — a whispered phone call, an unpaid bill,
  a name that doesn't match a face, a fishery that burns through more cash than it lands.

## What you're hiding (and your real job)
Dolan secretly hired you to **investigate his guests.** But here's how you work it, gumshoe:
your notebook holds **leads and questions, not confessions.** You don't *know* everyone's
secret — you know where the bodies *might* be buried, and you ask the right question at the
right moment until the guilty party says it themselves. You're the **pressure,** not the
oracle.

Your one weakness: pride. You're *itching* to prove the old Baltimore instincts haven't gone
soft.

## How to play it (important)
- **Don't announce secrets — pry them loose.** Instead of "Dave embezzled money," say "I hear
  you were on the horn to Atlanta last night, Big Daddy. Long call. What's eatin' you?" — and
  let *Dave* spill it.
- Across the night you can apply pressure on **any** guest using a vague lead from your
  notebook: the phone call (Dave), the missing grant money (Birdy), a name that isn't real
  (Walken), a guest who begged the old man for cash (Emma), accounts that don't add up (Ross),
  a "discovery" that smells fishy (Antoninski/Laurel), a prize that may be bought (Contessa).
- You also hold the hard evidence the case turns on — see your per-round handouts for **what
  you may reveal when** (the clean nightcap, the ledger, the glove, and the final search).
  Dole it out at dramatic moments. Don't shortcut to the solution.

## Your goal tonight
Solve the murder of the man who paid your wages. Use your leads and your evidence to crack
alibis and corner the killer. Prove the old gumshoe's still got it.

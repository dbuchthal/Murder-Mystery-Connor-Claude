# Buck Shanty
*"The Investigator" — a Baltimore gumshoe in a butler's coat*

## Who you are
The name's Buck Shanty. Twenty years on the gritty streets of Baltimore as a private detective
taught me three things: everybody lies, everybody's got an angle, and the quiet ones are the
worst. I hung up my trench coat and took a soft gig as Dr. Dolan's personal valet. Pressed his
suits. Brought his nightcap. Easy money.

Except it wasn't so easy. The old man was paranoid — and *right* to be. Now he's dead at his
desk, the storm's got the bridge in the drink and the phones cold, and there's a houseful of
swells who all wanted something from him. No cops coming. Somebody's got to work this case.
Lucky for the dear departed, he hired a detective.

## How you carry yourself
Clipped, hard-boiled, dimestore-noir narration. "The dame had a story. They always do." You're
observant to a fault and you let the silences do the heavy lifting.

## What you know about poison (and an awkward fact)
Twenty years of homicide work means you've **handled every poison in the evidence locker** — you
know the green ones, the slow ones, the ones that taste like almonds. Here's the awkward part,
gumshoe: **you personally carried Dolan his buttermilk nightcap** at ten o'clock. You *swear* it
was clean when you set it down — but you're the one soul who indisputably touched the fatal
glass, and a sharp guest may throw that in your face.

## The people here
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — your employer. You brought his nightcap at 10:00; it was clean.
- Everyone else is a person of interest. You've **watched** them for weeks on Dolan's orders, and
  your notebook's full of loose threads — a whispered call, an unpaid bill, a name that doesn't
  match a face, a fishery that burns more cash than it lands, a "discovery" that smells fishy.

## What you're hiding (and your real job)
Dolan secretly hired you to **investigate his guests.** But here's how you work it: your notebook
holds **leads and questions, not confessions.** You don't *know* everyone's secret — you know
where the bodies *might* be, and you ask the right question until the guilty say it themselves.
You're the **pressure,** not the oracle. (You've also **faked a reference or a document** or two
over the years to crack a case — so you're not lily-white either.)

## How to play it (important)
- **Don't announce secrets — pry them loose.** "I hear you were on the horn to Atlanta, Big
  Daddy. Long call. What's eatin' you?" — and let *Dave* spill it.
- Across the night, lean on **every** guest with a vague lead so each confesses their own poison
  access (R2) and their own fraud (R3). Keep the suspicion *spreading,* not narrowing.
- You hold the hard evidence the case finally turns on — see your per-round handouts for what you
  may reveal when (the clean nightcap, the ledger's *painting* forgery, and the Round 4 search).
  **Don't shortcut to the solution.**

## Your goal tonight
Solve the murder of the man who paid your wages — and clear your own name, since you're the one
who carried the glass. Prove the old Baltimore instincts haven't gone soft.

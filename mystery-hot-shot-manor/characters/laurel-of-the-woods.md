# Laurel of the Woods
*"The Moonshine Mama" — big jugs of brew and a head full of backwoods secrets*

## Who you are
Folks call you Laurel of the Woods, sugar, and there ain't a holler, roadhouse, or back-country
bar in these parts that don't light up when you roll in with your jugs of **potent moonshine.**
You were born to these forests; you know every deer trail and creek. That's how you caught the
eye of that Soviet fella, **Dr. Antoninski,** who hired you as his guide and tracker to hunt the
sasquatch.

Now the old crab-doctor's dead, the storm's washed out the bridge, and you're stuck inside with
a passel of city-folk who wrinkle their noses at your brew. Pity 'em, sugar.

## How you carry yourself
Twangy, folksy, sly as a fox. You sprinkle in hill-country proverbs ("that dog won't hunt,"
"slicker'n owl grease") and call everybody "sugar" or "city-folk." You play dumb, but you miss
*nothing.*

## Your special brew (and poison)
Your moonshine ain't just corn liquor — you cut it with homemade **green liqueurs,** wormwood and
herbs you distill yourself, potent enough to drop a moose. You know a heavy pour can poison as
easy as please. And here's the rub, sugar: at this very party, **one of your jugs of the strong
green stuff went MISSING** — somebody helped themselves. Which means your poison could be in
*anybody's* hands right now.

## The people here
- **Dr. Antoninski** — your employer and partner in a *particular* job (see below). With you in
  the woods last night. You've also seen him **squirrelin' away poison mushrooms** on your treks.
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — the other science fella. Didn't know him well.
- **Emma Lucas** — that uke star got a snootful of your green brew at a festival once.
- **Ross Coken** — carnival folk, like you've known all your life; you can smell a grifter's kit
  on him.
- The rest are strangers in fancy clothes.

## What you're hiding
Here's the big one, sugar: **you built the sasquatch hoax.** You sewed the suit, cast the
plaster prints, faked the howls — and you **doctored the photographs and cine-film** that
Antoninski submitted for his Nobel. You are a **hands-on manufacturer of fakes.** The
"discovery" that's about to win a prize is *your forgery.*

Last night, from about **10:15 to 11:30 PM,** you and Antoninski were in the woods staging a
fresh "sighting." You can vouch for him — but you're his only alibi, and folks don't trust a
known faker.

## Your goal tonight
Keep the fake-sasquatch racket buried (your livelihood and his Nobel depend on it). Mind that
your stolen jug doesn't get pinned on you. Don't get hanged for a killin' you didn't do.

## Threads you can pull
- You've seen **Antoninski forage poison mushrooms** — if folks hunt for who knows green poison,
  **point at the Soviet** before it lands on your jugs.
- You can smell **Ross's** old carnie poison kit a mile off — **call him out** if you need to
  spread suspicion.

# Laurel of the Woods
*"The Moonshine Mama" — big jugs of brew and a head full of backwoods secrets*

## Who you are
Folks call you Laurel of the Woods, sugar, and there ain't a holler, roadhouse, or
back-country bar in these parts that don't light up when you roll in with your jugs of
**potent moonshine.** You were born to these forests; you know every deer trail, every creek,
every place a body could hide. That's how you caught the eye of that Soviet fella, **Dr.
Antoninski,** who hired you on as his guide and tracker to hunt the sasquatch.

Now the old crab-doctor's gone and died up at the big house, the storm's washed out the
bridge, and you're stuck inside with a passel of city-folk who wrinkle their noses at your
brew. Pity 'em, sugar. They don't know what they're missin'.

## How you carry yourself
Twangy, folksy, sly as a fox. You sprinkle in hill-country proverbs ("that dog won't hunt,"
"slicker'n owl grease") and call everybody "sugar" or "city-folk." You play dumb, but you miss
*nothing.*

## Your special brew (and poison)
Your moonshine ain't just corn liquor, sugar — you cut it with homemade **green liqueurs,**
wormwood and herb infusions you distill yourself. The green stuff is potent enough to drop a
moose, and you know full well that a heavy pour can poison as easy as please. You're proud of
it. You'd just rather the law didn't hear how *potent* it gets.

## The people here
- **Dr. Antoninski** — your employer and your partner in a *particular* job (see below). You
  were out in the woods with him last night. You watch his back; he pays well. You've also
  seen him **squirreling away mushrooms** and jarring up extracts on your treks — that man
  knows his poison fungi.
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — the other science fella, with his crabs. Didn't know him well.
- **Emma Lucas** — that uke star. You crossed paths at a festival years back; she got a snootful
  of your green brew once and still talks about it, you'd bet.
- The rest are strangers in fancy clothes.

## What you're hiding
Here's the big one, sugar: **you built the fake sasquatch.** You sewed the suit, you cast the
plaster footprints, you faked the howls — the whole hoax, for Antoninski's good money. The
"discovery" that's about to win him a Nobel is *your handiwork.*

Last night, from about **10:15 to 11:30 PM,** you and Antoninski were out in the woods staging
a fresh sasquatch "sighting" for the Contessa. You were together the whole time — you can clear
him, but only by admitting the hoax.

## Your goal tonight
Keep the fake-sasquatch racket buried (your livelihood and Antoninski's Nobel depend on it).
Decide when to use the woods alibi. Don't get pinned for a killin' you didn't do.

## A thread you can pull
You've seen **Antoninski foraging poison mushrooms** out on the trail. If folk start huntin'
for who knows green poisons, you can point out that **the Soviet's a mushroom man** — turn the
spotlight toward him before it lands on your jugs.

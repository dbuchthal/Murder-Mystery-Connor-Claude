# Gwen Buchthal
*"The Georgia Peach" — Southern sugar wrapped around a switchblade*

## Who you are
You are Gwen Buchthal, and *honey*, you have arrived. Two years ago you were just another
Atlanta peach; tonight you sit on the board of the Seattle Art Museum and the whole Emerald
City wants you at their parties. You came west with your devoted husband Dave ("Big Daddy")
when Coca-Cola sent him to open the Seattle plant, and you've been climbing the social ladder
two rungs at a time ever since — in heels.

Your great public project has been charming Dr. Connor Dolan into donating the crown jewels
of his art collection to the museum. You've smiled at that paranoid old crab for months. *"My
peaches, what a sight!"*

Now Dolan is dead, the bridge is washed out, the phones are kaput, and a houseful of
glittering vultures is eyeing each other over the cooling body. You intend to be the picture
of grace, grief, and innocence. Naturally.

## How you carry yourself
Honeyed Georgia drawl, never a hair out of place. You deflect with compliments and bury
knives in "bless your heart." You can dab a tear and change the subject in the same breath.
When cornered, you get *sweeter,* not sharper. Catchphrase: *"My peaches, what a sight!"*

## The people here
- **Dave,** your husband — loud, loyal, and pleasingly oblivious. Keep him close.
- **Buck Shanty,** the valet — too quiet, too watchful. You caught him **eyeing the guests'
  luggage** this morning, and you do not like it one bit. What is a valet doing pawing
  through people's bags?
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — the man you spent months flattering. You will mourn him
  *beautifully.*
- The rest — **Emma, Ross, Walken, the Contessa, Antoninski, Laurel, Birdy** — a circus of
  has-beens and hangers-on. You know them only as faces at the party. Whatever each of them
  is hiding, you've no idea — yet.

## What you're hiding — READ CAREFULLY, YOU ARE THE KILLER
**You murdered Dr. Connor Dolan.** Here is the truth no one else knows:

For two years you have been **forging his art collection** — quietly selling his genuine
masterpieces on the black market and hanging flawless fakes in their place. That black-market
money bought your way into Seattle society. You are not an art donor; you are an art *thief,*
and a trained restorer skilled enough to fake a master.

Dolan **found out.** Last night he summoned you to his study, showed you the proof in his
private ledger, and said he'd name you at tomorrow's press conference — ruining you, jailing
you, ending everything. So at about **10:50 PM** (telling Dave you had a headache and were
going to bed) you went to his study and, while you "made peace," you stirred a measure of
**Paris Green** — the emerald, arsenical pigment from your **art-restoration kit** — into his
nightly buttermilk. He drank around 11:00. You slipped out. He died by 11:15.

Two mistakes you don't yet realize are problems: you **dropped a peach-colored glove
monogrammed "G.B."** near the study door, and you left a faint **greenish smear** of pigment
on the desk and glass. Your restoration kit (with the Paris Green) is still in your luggage.

**What you must conceal:** that you forge art, that you were in the study, that you own
arsenical pigments, and that your "headache / early to bed" alibi is a lie. Push the line that
poor Connor's *bad heart* simply gave out.

## Playing the killer
Stay **calm.** Grieve prettily. Deflect with charm, never anger. **Lie consistently** — your
per-round handouts will tell you exactly what's been exposed each round and what to keep
denying, so your story never cracks on its own. Don't invent accusations about other guests'
secrets — you don't know them; let the others tear each other apart on their own. Your job is
to stay *boring and bereaved.*

## Your goal tonight
Survive the night un-accused. Keep attention on Dolan's "weak heart" and off the study, the
glove, and your luggage. If anyone proposes searching the guests' bags — *redirect, sweetly.*

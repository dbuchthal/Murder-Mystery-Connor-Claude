# Gwen Buchthal
*"The Georgia Peach" — Southern sugar wrapped around a switchblade*

## Who you are
You are Gwen Buchthal, and *honey,* you have arrived. Two years ago you were just another
Atlanta peach; tonight you sit on the board of the Seattle Art Museum and the whole Emerald
City wants you at their parties. You came west with your devoted husband Dave ("Big Daddy")
when Coca-Cola sent him to open the Seattle plant, and you've been climbing the social ladder
two rungs at a time ever since — in heels.

You're also a **trained art restorer** — you can clean a varnish, reline a canvas, and mix
your own pigments with a master's hand. Your public project has been charming Dr. Connor Dolan
into donating his collection to the museum. You've smiled at that paranoid old crab for months.
*"My peaches, what a sight!"*

Now Dolan is dead, the bridge is washed out, the phones are kaput, and a houseful of glittering
vultures is eyeing each other over the cooling body. You intend to be the picture of grace,
grief, and innocence. Naturally.

## How you carry yourself
Honeyed Georgia drawl, never a hair out of place. You deflect with compliments and bury knives
in "bless your heart." When cornered, you get *sweeter,* not sharper. *"My peaches, what a
sight!"*

## The people here
- **Dave,** your husband — loud, loyal, pleasingly oblivious. Keep him close.
- **Buck Shanty,** the valet — too quiet, too watchful. You caught him **eyeing the guests'
  luggage** *and* fussing over Dolan's tray this morning. What kind of valet does that?
- **Dr. Dolan** (deceased) — the man you spent months flattering. Mourn him *beautifully.*
- The rest are faces at a party. Whatever each of them is hiding, you've no idea — yet.

## What you're hiding — READ CAREFULLY, YOU ARE THE KILLER
**You murdered Dr. Connor Dolan.** The truth no one else knows:

For two years you have been **forging his paintings** — quietly selling his genuine
masterpieces and hanging your own flawless **painted fakes** in their place. That black-market
money bought your way into Seattle society. You are not an art donor; you are a painter of
forgeries, skilled enough to fool the world.

Dolan **found out.** Last night he showed you the proof in his private ledger and said he'd
name you at tomorrow's press conference — ruining you, jailing you. So at about **10:50 PM**
(telling Dave you had a headache and were going to bed) you slipped into his study and, while
you "made peace," you stirred a measure of **Paris Green** — the emerald, arsenical **oil-paint
pigment** from your **restoration kit** — into his nightly buttermilk. He drank around 11:00.
You slipped out. He died by 11:15.

**What you must conceal — in two layers:**
- **Layer one (guard loosely):** that you own restorer's chemicals and that you're an art-world
  insider who *could* fake a canvas. If pressed, you can admit to the *generic* stuff — it makes
  you one faker among many.
- **Layer two (guard with your life):** that you forge actual **paintings,** and that your kit
  holds **Paris Green.** The poison was a *paint pigment.* If anyone fuses "painting forgery"
  with "paint poison," they have you. **Never** let those two ideas touch.

## Playing the killer
Stay **calm.** Grieve prettily. Deflect with charm, never anger. **Lie consistently** — your
per-round handouts tell you exactly what's exposed each round and what to keep denying. You're
lucky: *everyone* here turns out to have a poison and a fraud in their past, so let them tear
each other apart. Your job is to be one suspect in a crowd of ten — never *the* one.

## Your goal tonight
Survive un-accused. Keep "painting forgery" and "paint poison" in separate rooms. If anyone
proposes searching the guests' luggage, *redirect, sweetly.*

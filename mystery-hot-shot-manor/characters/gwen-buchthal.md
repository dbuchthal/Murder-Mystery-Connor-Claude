# Gwen Buchthal
*"The Georgia Peach" — Southern sugar wrapped around a switchblade*

## Who you are
You are Gwen Buchthal, and *honey*, you have arrived. Two years ago you were just another
Atlanta peach; tonight you sit on the board of the Seattle Art Museum and the whole
Emerald City wants you at their parties. You came west with your devoted husband Dave
("Big Daddy") when Coca-Cola sent him to open the Seattle plant, and you have been climbing
the social ladder two rungs at a time ever since — in heels.

Your great public project has been charming Dr. Connor Dolan into donating the crown
jewels of his art collection to the museum. Such a gift would make you *the* name in
Pacific Northwest culture. You've smiled at that paranoid old crab for months. *"My
peaches, what a sight!"*

Now Dolan is dead, the bridge is washed out, the phones are kaput, and a houseful of
glittering vultures is eyeing each other over the cooling body. You intend to be the
picture of grace, grief, and innocence. Naturally.

## How you carry yourself
Honeyed Georgia drawl, never a hair out of place. You deflect with compliments and bury
knives in "bless your heart." You can dab a tear and change the subject in the same breath.
When cornered, you get *sweeter*, not sharper. Catchphrase: *"My peaches, what a sight!"*

## The people here
- **Dave**, your husband — loud, loyal, and pleasingly oblivious. Keep him close.
- **Dr. Antoninski** — sweaty and shifty tonight. Wouldn't it be *convenient* if everyone
  looked at him?
- **Birdy Robbins** — that fish-stink boat woman was creeping around the house last night.
  Watch her.
- **Buck Shanty**, the valet — too quiet, too watchful. You don't like the way he looks at
  luggage.
- **Christopher Walken, Emma, Ross, the Contessa, Laurel** — a circus of has-beens and
  hangers-on, each hiding something. Use them.

## What you're hiding — READ CAREFULLY, YOU ARE THE KILLER
**You murdered Dr. Connor Dolan.** Here is the truth no one else knows:

For two years you have been **forging his art collection** — quietly selling his genuine
masterpieces on the black market and hanging flawless fakes in their place. That black-
market money is what bought your way into Seattle society. You are not an art donor; you
are an art *thief*.

Dolan **found out.** Last night he summoned you to his study, showed you the proof in his
private ledger, and told you he would name you at tomorrow's press conference — ruining you,
jailing you, ending everything. So at about **10:50 PM** you went to his study (telling Dave
you had a headache and were going to bed), and while you "made peace," you stirred **Paris
Green** — the emerald arsenical pigment from your **art-restoration kit** — into his nightly
buttermilk. He drank it around 11:00. You slipped out. He died by 11:15.

Two mistakes, which you don't yet know are problems: you **dropped a peach-colored glove
monogrammed "G.B."** near the study door, and you left a faint **green pigment smudge** on
the desk and glass. Your restoration kit (with the Paris Green) is still in your luggage.

**What you must conceal:** that you forge art, that you were in the study, that you own
arsenical pigments, and that your "headache / early to bed" alibi is a lie. Push the line
that poor Connor's *bad heart* finally gave out — natural causes.

## Playing the killer
Stay **calm**. Grieve prettily. Deflect with charm, never with anger. Point the table
gently toward Antoninski (he looks guilty all on his own). **Lie consistently** — your
per-round handouts will tell you exactly what's been exposed each round and what to keep
denying, so your story never cracks on its own. If they find the glove, it was "stolen." If
they mention pigments, you've "never restored a thing in your life, sugar."

## Your goal tonight
Survive the night un-accused. Steer suspicion onto anyone else — Antoninski first. Get the
group to rule it "natural causes" or pin it on a rival before anyone searches the luggage.

## What you don't know
You don't know that **Birdy saw you** leaving the study wing at 11:00. You don't know that
**Buck was secretly hired to investigate the guests** and keeps a notebook of everyone's
secrets — including, eventually, yours. You don't know the others' specific secrets
(Dave's embezzlement, the faked sasquatch, the Contessa's bribe, Emma being broke) until
they surface in play.

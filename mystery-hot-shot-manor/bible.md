# Murder at Hot Shot Manor — Story Bible

> **HOST ONLY — do not show to players.** This is the complete ground truth. Every
> character background, round handout, and narrator script is a *view* onto this file.
> If a fact isn't here, it isn't true.
>
> **Design rules enforced in this version (read before running):**
> 1. **Strict fog-of-war.** No player document tells a character anything they don't already
>    know. Backgrounds have **no "What you don't know" section.** A secret of character X never
>    appears on character Y's sheet until the round *after* X reveals it in play.
> 2. **Secrets are surfaced by OTHER players.** Every secret is **questioned by ≥2 different
>    characters** (each given a legitimate *hook*, never the secret itself) and is **revealed by
>    the holder** when questioned (§7c).
> 3. **The murder method is concealed until R4.** Before Round 4 the poison is referred to
>    **only** as "a green poisonous substance." The term **"Paris Green"** (and "pigment,"
>    "arsenic," "paint poison") appears **only in Round 4.**
> 4. **EVERYONE is a viable suspect.** By the end of Round 3, every living guest has had
>    revealed: (a) **access to a poison**, (b) **a fraud/forgery connection**, (c) a plausible
>    **motive**, and (d) a **murky, uncorroborated opportunity.** There is **no clean alibi for
>    anyone** and **no single obvious clue** pointing at the killer.
> 5. **NO glove, NO naming eyewitness.** Those clues are deleted. The **only** path to the
>    killer is the **intersection of art-forgery and poison**, which fuses uniquely in Round 4:
>    the poison is an *oil-paint pigment* and the forgery Dolan caught was *painting* forgery —
>    so the killer is the one who forges **paintings** and kills with **paint.** That is Gwen,
>    and only Gwen.

## 1. Premise & setting

**Hot Shot Manor, La Conner, Washington. The dead of night, autumn 1989.** Rain threatening
but not yet broken. The manor belongs to **Dr. Connor Dolan**, world-renowned cryptozoologist,
eccentric trillionaire-of-the-tide-pools, and the only man alive who claims to have shaken
hands (claws?) with the aquatic **crab-people of the San Juan Islands**.

Dolan summoned a houseful of glittering, grasping guests for a weekend "announcement gala" —
he promised to reveal something that would "rearrange the furniture of several lives." Then,
before he could say a word, he turned up **dead at his desk**, slumped over his beloved
**cryptex safe**, the one holding his freshly rewritten will. A coastal storm has knocked out
the bridge and the phones. Nobody leaves. Nobody calls the cops.

Somebody at this party is a killer. **Everybody** at this party is a liar, a faker, and — it
turns out — somebody with poison in their bag.

**Tone:** a 1920s film-noir potboiler that fell through a wormhole into 1989 — smoke-machine
atmosphere and hard-boiled narration, stuffed with Reagan-era slang, neon, synthesizers, VHS,
and shoulder pads. Campy, absurdist, knowing. The narrator is a gravel-voiced gumshoe who keeps
getting distracted by the decade.

## 2. Cast

### Dr. Connor Dolan — "Dead Man Walking" — THE VICTIM
- **Role:** Host. Cryptozoologist obsessed with the crab-people. Old, ailing, paranoid, rich.
- **Public persona:** A genius gone to seed, convinced everyone was robbing him — correctly.
- **Private secret:** He had **proof of an art-forgery scheme** in his private ledger:
  genuine pieces of his collection had been **repainted/swapped for fakes** — *painting*
  forgery. He'd scheduled a press conference to name the forger and rewrote his estate.
- **Voice:** Querulous, grandiose, paranoid. Dead — relevant as backstory + ghost role (§9).

### Gwen Buchthal — "The Georgia Peach" — **THE KILLER**
- **Role:** Atlanta transplant, rising Seattle socialite, Seattle Art Museum board member, and
  a **trained art restorer** who grinds and mixes her own pigments.
- **Public persona:** Southern charm; petitioning Dolan to donate art to the museum.
  *"My peaches, what a sight!"*
- **Poison access:** **(HIDDEN UNTIL R4)** her restoration kit holds **Paris Green** — an
  emerald, arsenical **oil-paint pigment.** *Publicly (R2) she only admits to owning generic
  "restorer's solvents and chemicals" — never the pigment.*
- **Fraud/forgery angle:** **She is the real forger.** For two years she has **forged Dolan's
  paintings** — selling the originals, hanging her own painted fakes. *(R3 she admits only to
  being an art-world insider/restorer "who could spot or fix a canvas" — the painting-forgery
  truth is hers alone and is proven against her in R4.)*
- **Private secret (THE MURDER):** Dolan caught the painting swaps and threatened to expose her.
  At ~10:50 PM she stirred **Paris Green** into his buttermilk. Motive: survival.
- **Voice:** Honeyed Georgia drawl, lethal under the sugar.

- **Fraud/forgery angle:** he's been **cooking Coca-Cola's books** and **forging financials and

- **Voice:** Clipped hard-boiled Baltimore PI. "The dame had a story. They always do."

## 3. The crime

- **Victim:** Dr. Connor Dolan.
- **Method:** Poisoning.
- **Weapon/means (HOST-ONLY until R4):** **Paris Green** — emerald, arsenical **oil-paint
  pigment** from Gwen's restoration kit — stirred into his nightly **buttermilk.** *Before R4,
  to players, only "a green poisonous substance."*
- **Location:** Dolan's private study, at the cryptex desk.
- **Time of death:** ~11:15 PM (drank the laced nightcap ~11:00 PM).
- **Killer:** Gwen Buchthal.
- **Motive:** Dolan discovered she'd been **forging his paintings** and threatened to expose
  her at the next day's press conference.

## 4. True timeline (the previous night)

- **8:30 PM** — Guests assemble; storm rolling in; bridge will wash out.
- **9:00 PM** — Dinner. Dolan announces he'll "name a thief and a forger among you tomorrow"
  and reveal a rewritten will; insults nearly everyone; locks the will in the study cryptex.
- **10:00 PM** — Dolan retires to the study. **Buck** brings the buttermilk, sets it down,
  leaves. (It is **clean.**)
- **10:15–10:50 PM** — Everyone scatters into the dark, stormy house and grounds. **No one has
  a corroborated location** for the whole window:
  - **Antoninski + Laurel** slip to the woods to fake a sasquatch sighting (they alibi *each
    other* — two proven liars). Laurel later finds a **jug of her green brew missing.**
  - **Contessa** in and out of her room and the parlor.
  - **Emma** wasted in her room; **Ross** in and out "tending" her (each alibis the other —
    one wasted, one a con man).
  - **Dave** on a long Atlanta phone call (~10:30–11:20) — but **no one saw him**; the line was
    in a hall nook.
  - **Walken** performing in the parlor — but he **slipped out** between numbers; witnesses
    fuzzy.
  - **Birdy** at the docks (alone, unverified) — and **back in the house ~11:00** near the
    study wing.
  - **Buck** turned in after the nightcap (unverified) — and he's the one who *handled the
    glass.*
  - Guests were also seen drifting near **Birdy's boat** earlier; **Laurel's missing jug**
    floats somewhere in the house.
- **10:50 PM** — **Gwen** ("headache, going to bed") slips into the study with her restoration
  kit, stirs **Paris Green** into the buttermilk while "making peace."
- **11:00 PM** — Dolan drinks. Gwen leaves, leaving a faint **greenish residue** on the glass
  (generic poison trace — points to no one). **Birdy** glimpses *a figure* leaving the wing —
  unidentifiable.
- **11:15 PM** — Dolan dies at his desk.
- **~7:00 AM (game start)** — **Buck** finds the body, locks the study, gathers the guests.

**Consistency check:** Everyone's opportunity is plausible and uncorroborated; no two people
are pinned to the same place. The killer's window is *not* exclusive on opportunity grounds —
that is intentional. Gwen is singled out **only** by the art-forgery × paint-poison fusion (§6).

## 6. Solution clue-chain (the ONLY path: art-forgery × poison)

Three key clues. The first two are widely shared (everyone fakes, everyone has poison); the
**fusion** in clue 3 is unique to Gwen.

1. **THE FORGERY DOLAN CAUGHT WAS *PAINTING* FORGERY (motive).** The will (R1) and ledger (R2)
   say a guest **repainted and swapped his canvases.** Many guests are *fakers* (film, papers,
   provenance, finance, identity, certification) — but the ledger is specific: **someone forged
   his paintings.** Corroborated by **Walken's** connoisseur eye (R3): a canvas was **painted
   over.** *Points at: whoever can actually paint.*
2. **THE POISON WAS A GREEN SUBSTANCE (R2), identified in R4 as PARIS GREEN — an OIL-PAINT
   PIGMENT.** Everyone has *a* poison, so "green poison" alone clears no one. But its **identity
   as a painter's pigment** (R4) is the tell: the killer poisoned Dolan with **paint.**
3. **THE INTERSECTION + THE KIT (R4).** Combine 1 and 2: the murderer **forges paintings** *and*
   **kills with paint.** Only **Gwen** — a trained restorer who grinds her own pigments — does
   both. Buck's R4 search finds **her restoration kit containing the matching Paris Green.**
   Every other suspect's fakery is *not painting* and every other poison is *not paint.*

**Intended deduction (R4):** "The weapon was a painter's emerald pigment. The forgery Dolan
uncovered was *painting* forgery. The murderer used the very medium of their crime to kill him.
Among ten fakers with ten poisons, exactly one forges canvases and works in paint — **Gwen
Buchthal.**" No glove, no eyewitness, no alibi-elimination — purely the art × poison fusion.

**Why no one else fits (host quick-reference):** Kristof/Laurel fake *film & sculpture*
(poison: mushroom/liqueur); Ross fakes *provenance/papers* (knockout drops); Emma *fences*
others' art (pharmacy); Walken fakes *identity/fashion* and only *spots* the bad canvas
(FX chemicals); Contessa *certifies* fakes (tincture); Dave forges *documents/finance*
(plant chemicals); Birdy *smuggles* art (kelp); Buck fakes *references* (evidence poisons).
None forge *paintings*; none poison with *paint.* Only Gwen sits in both circles.

## 8. Round plan

- **R1 — The Gathering & the Cryptex.** Pool ten clues → `CRABBY`; open the cryptex; read the
  will (painting-forgery motive seeded; suspicion wide). Meet everyone.
- **R2 — Everybody's Got Poison.** Coroner's note: a green poison. Round-robin, every guest is
  outed as having access to *some* poison (and Laurel's stolen jug / Birdy's lurkers mean
  anyone could've borrowed another's). Buck shows the ledger: the forgery was *painting*
  forgery. Suspicion *maximizes.*
- **R3 — Everybody's a Faker.** Every guest is outed as some kind of fraud/forger; alibis all
  collapse into mutual mistrust; Kristof is hard-pressed as the poisoner; Walken confirms a
  canvas was painted over. **End: no clear suspect — false leads point at all ten.**
- **R4 — The Fusion.** Vote; identify the poison as **Paris Green (oil paint)**; recall the
  ledger's *painting* forgery; show only Gwen sits in both circles; produce her kit; reveal;
  resolve every false lead.

## 10. The Cryptex Puzzle (Round 1 mechanic)

**Answer word: `CRABBY`** (Dolan's temperament + his crab-people). Six letter-clues + four
meta-clues; all ten guests hold a fragment.

**Letter clues (position → letter):**
1. **Christopher Walken** → *"The first letter is... the third star... in the alphabet's
   parade. Wow."* → **1 = C**
2. **Ross Coken** → *"Second slot — the 18th letter, the one that rolls off a Scotsman's
   tongue: rrrr."* → **2 = R**
3. **Dr. Antoninski** → *"The third is the first. The alpha. In Soviet alphabet, also first:
   A."* → **3 = A**
4. **Birdy Robbins** → *"Fourth slot — the letter that buoys a boat and starts my own name,
   Birdy. A big ol' B."* → **4 = B**
5. **Buck Shanty** → *"Position five, doll — same letter as four. History repeats. B, like
   Buck, like Baltimore."* → **5 = B**
6. **Laurel of the Woods** → *"Last one, sugar — the letter that asks a question and ends 'em
   too: Y."* → **6 = Y**

**Meta-clues:**
7. **Gwen** → *"Six letters long — no more, no less, my peaches."*
8. **Dave** → *"One letter shows up twice — sweet sassafras, a repeater!"*
9. **Contessa** → *"It describes the doctor's temperament — and his precious sea-creatures.
   Tutti crabby, no?"*
10. **Emma** → *"Cosmic vision, man... starts hard, ends with a question-mark of a letter.
    C... to... Y."*

**Host mechanic:** hand each player their fragment (bottom of their R1 handout); assemble
`CRABBY`; open the cryptex; read the will (R1 narrator). If brute-forced, read the will and
proceed — no murder clue is gated behind the puzzle.

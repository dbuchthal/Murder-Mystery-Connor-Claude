## 1. Premise & setting

**Hot Shot Manor, La Conner, Washington. The dead of night, autumn 1989.** Rain is
threatening but hasn't broken — yet. The manor belongs to **Dr. Connor Dolan**,
world-renowned cryptozoologist, eccentric trillionaire-of-the-tide-pools, and the only
man alive who claims to have shaken hands (claws?) with the aquatic **crab-people of the
San Juan Islands**.

Dolan summoned a houseful of glittering, grasping guests for a weekend "announcement
gala" — he promised to reveal something that would "rearrange the furniture of several
lives." Then, before he could say a word, he turned up **dead at his desk**, slumped over
his beloved **cryptex safe**, the one holding his freshly rewritten will. A coastal storm
has knocked out the bridge and the phones. Nobody leaves. Nobody calls the cops.

Somebody at this party is a killer. Everybody at this party is a liar.

**Tone:** play it like a 1920s film-noir potboiler that fell through a wormhole into
1989 — smoke-machine atmosphere and hard-boiled narration, but stuffed with Reagan-era
slang, neon, synthesizers, VHS tapes, and shoulder pads. Campy, absurdist, knowing. The
narrator is a gravel-voiced gumshoe who keeps getting distracted by the decade.

## 2. Cast

### Dr. Connor Dolan — "Dead Man Walking" — THE VICTIM
- **Role:** The host. Cryptozoologist; obsessed with contacting the crab-people. Old,
  ailing, paranoid, fabulously rich.
- **Public persona:** A genius gone to seed. Recently convinced everyone was stealing
  from him — and, as it turns out, several of them were.
- **Private secret:** He had **proof of an art forgery ring** operating right under his
  nose. His private ledger documented that pieces of his celebrated art collection had
  been quietly swapped for fakes. He'd scheduled a press conference to name the thief and
  was rewriting his entire estate to punish the people who'd been bleeding him.
- **Relationships:** Patron, mark, or obstacle to nearly everyone here. Adored Emma
  Lucas's music. Funneled grant money to Birdy. "Friendly rival" of Antoninski. Courted
  by Gwen for an art donation and by Dave for a land sale. Employed Buck as valet.
- **Voice:** Querulous, grandiose, paranoid. Refers to himself in the third person. Dead,
  so mostly relevant as backstory and as the ghost-helper role (see §9).

### Gwen Buchthal — "The Georgia Peach" — **THE KILLER**
- **Role:** Atlanta transplant, rising star of Seattle high society, board member of the
  Seattle Art Museum.
- **Public persona:** All Southern charm and biting wit. Petitioning Dolan to donate
  prize pieces of his collection to the museum — a coup that would cement her social
  ascent. Catchphrase: *"My peaches, what a sight!"*
- **Private secret (THE MURDER):** Gwen is the forger. For two years she has been
  quietly selling off Dolan's genuine masterpieces on the black market and hanging
  flawless fakes in their place, bankrolling the lifestyle that bought her way into
  Seattle society. Dolan **discovered the swap** and confronted her privately last night,
  threatening to name her at tomorrow's press conference — which would obliterate her.
  She killed him with **Paris Green** — the arsenical emerald pigment from her art-
  restoration kit — stirred into his nightly buttermilk.
- **Relationships:** Married to Dave (who knows nothing of the forgery). Rival-friend to
  every art-and-money person in the room. Publicly fawning over Dolan; privately
  terrified of him.
- **Voice:** Honeyed Georgia drawl, lethal under the sugar. Deflects with charm and
  compliments. "Bless your heart" is a weapon.

### Dave Buchthal — "The Big Daddy"
- **Role:** Coca-Cola VP from Atlanta, in town to open a Seattle bottling plant.
- **Public persona:** Cigar-chomping good-ol'-boy dealmaker. Was negotiating to buy a
  big La Conner land parcel from Dolan. Catchphrase: *"Sweet sassafras, I feel it deep in
  my plums!"*
- **Private secret:** The Seattle plant is a **financial disaster** and Dave has been
  **cooking the books**, quietly moving Coca-Cola relocation funds to cover his own
  losses. He desperately needed Dolan to sign the land deal — it was the only thing that
  could've made the numbers work. **He needed Dolan alive.**
- **Relationships:** Gwen's devoted husband; genuinely doesn't know she's the forger or
  the killer. Resented Dolan's stalling.
- **Voice:** Booming, folksy, sweaty bravado. Bourbon-and-molasses metaphors.

### Emma Lucas — "The Uke of Hazard"
- **Role:** World-famous (1980s) ukulele pop star, mid-controversial pivot to the
  "electric synth-uke." Band on hiatus.
- **Public persona:** Dolan's favorite musician; a burned-out icon on a "drug-fueled
  journey of self-discovery."
- **Private secret:** She's **broke**. The synth-uke flopped, the tour hemorrhaged money,
  and she came to the gala to **beg Dolan for a loan**. Humiliating. She was also genuinely
  wasted last night.
- **Relationships:** Adored by Dolan; tended (and quietly fleeced) by Ross. Resents that
  Ross knows how broke she is.
- **Voice:** Spacey, grandiose, name-drops cities and festivals. Drifts off mid-sentence.

### Ross Coken — "Remora Ross"
- **Role:** Scottish ex-carnie contortionist; the world's most famous rock groupie,
  attached to Emma.
- **Public persona:** Charming hanger-on, professional muse and gofer.
- **Private secret:** Now that the tour's on hiatus and Emma's broke, Ross has been
  **skimming from Emma's accounts** for months, and was already shopping around for his
  next "host" — possibly Dolan himself.
- **Relationships:** Latched onto Emma; alibis her for last night (they were together).
  Eyeing Dolan's fortune.
- **Voice:** Thick Scottish patter, carnival-barker flourishes, bends the truth like he
  bends his spine.

### Christopher Walken — "Walken on Sunshine"
- **Role:** A-list Hollywood actor, fashion icon, dancer; key investor in Dolan's
  research.
- **Public persona:** Loves reminding people he "used to be kind of a big deal" in
  Seattle fashion. Distinctive... halting... way of... speaking.
- **Private secret:** "Walken" is a stage name — he's really **Christopher Chappman**,
  and his finances are quietly collapsing. He **secretly withdrew his investment** in
  Dolan's crab-people research weeks ago and has been dreading Dolan finding out.
- **Relationships:** Celebrity friend of Dolan; performative rival of everyone for

- **Voice:** The Walken cadence. Strange. Pauses. Where you... wouldn't. Expect them.

### Ms. Jay "Birdy" Robbins — "The Canary in the Coal Mine"
- **Role:** Owner of the Red Irish Lord fishery; Dolan's offshore research contractor.
- **Public persona:** Salt-crusted boat captain. Dolan funneled a fortune in grant money
  to her "research fleet."
- **Private secret:** The "research vessels" are partly a **smuggling front**, and Birdy
  has been **pocketing grant money** the IRS is now sniffing at. An audit would sink her —
  so she **needed Dolan alive** to keep the money (and the cover story) flowing.
- **Relationships:** Financially dependent on Dolan. Was down at the docks last night —
  and, returning for forgotten keys, **saw Gwen leaving the study wing at 11pm.**
- **Voice:** Gruff, nautical, deadpan. Curses like a sailor, then catches herself.

### Dr. Kristof Antoninski — "The Severe Soviet"
- **Role:** Cryptozoologist from the University of Moscow; Dolan's "friendly rival."
  Front-runner for the 1989 Nobel for his sasquatch "discovery."
- **Public persona:** Severe, humorless, triumphant. The man who "found" the sasquatch.
- **Private secret:** The sasquatch discovery is **completely faked** — costume, plaster
  footprints, the works — and **Dolan knew it** and was going to expose him. Antoninski's
  whole Nobel rests on a lie. (Strong-looking motive — but see §5.)
- **Relationships:** Bitter rival of Dolan; employer of Laurel (who helped fake the
  evidence); courting Contessa's favorable judgment.
- **Voice:** Clipped, grim, dramatic. Overpronounces. "In Soviet cryptozoology..."

### Contessa Rebecca Savoca — "Italian Nobel-ity"
- **Role:** Head of the Nobel Committee on Cryptozoology; runs the Savoca Institute in
  Sicily. Her verdict decides the prize.
- **Public persona:** Imperious noblewoman, final arbiter of scientific glory.
- **Private secret:** The Savoca Institute is **bankrupt**, and she has accepted a large
  **bribe from Antoninski** to rule in his favor. If the sasquatch fraud surfaces, she's
  ruined too.
- **Relationships:** Compromised by Antoninski; coolly dismissive of Dolan's crab-people.
  Retired to her room early but was in and out.
- **Voice:** Haughty, continental, drips condescension. Italian endearments and insults.

### Laurel of the Woods — "The Moonshine Mama"
- **Role:** Backwoods moonshiner; hired by Antoninski as wilderness guide and tracker.
- **Public persona:** Folksy hill-country fixture with jugs of potent brew.
- **Private secret:** She **built the fake sasquatch** — sewed the suit, cast the
  footprints, faked the howls — for Antoninski's money. She knows the whole fraud. Her
  moonshine is also strong enough to drop a moose (a **red herring** about the poison).
- **Relationships:** On Antoninski's payroll; **alibis him** for 11pm (they snuck into
  the woods to stage a fresh "sighting" for the Contessa).
- **Voice:** Twangy, sly, folksy proverbs. Calls everyone "sugar" or "city-folk."

### Buck Shanty — "The Investigator"
- **Role:** Retired Baltimore private eye, now Dolan's personal valet.
- **Public persona:** Stone-faced manservant; last person to see Dolan alive (delivered
  the nightcap).
- **Private secret:** Dolan secretly hired Buck **to investigate the guests** and dig up
  dirt — Buck has a notebook full of everyone's secrets. He's not a suspect so much as the
  table's **engine of clues**: he can corroborate the autopsy and produce evidence when
  pressed. (His one vanity: he's desperate to prove he's "still got it" as a detective.)
- **Relationships:** Loyal to Dolan; quietly investigating everyone. The host's best
  friend at the table.
- **Voice:** Clipped hard-boiled Baltimore PI. Talks like dimestore noir. "The dame had a
  story. They always do."

## 3. The crime

- **Victim:** Dr. Connor Dolan.
- **Method:** Poisoning.
- **Weapon/means:** **Paris Green** — emerald-green arsenical pigment used in oil
  painting and art restoration — stirred into Dolan's nightly glass of **buttermilk**
  (his eccentric nightcap). A teaspoon was lethal given his already-failing health.
- **Location:** Dolan's private study, at the desk where the cryptex safe sits.
- **Time of death:** ~11:15 PM (he drank the laced nightcap around 11:00 PM).
- **Killer:** Gwen Buchthal.
- **Motive:** Dolan discovered Gwen had been forging and selling his art collection. He
  confronted her and threatened to expose her at the next day's press conference —
  destroying her social ascent and sending her to prison. She silenced him.

## 4. True timeline (the previous night)

- **8:30 PM** — Guests assemble at Hot Shot Manor for Dolan's gala. Storm rolling in; the
  bridge will wash out overnight, stranding everyone.
- **9:00 PM** — Dinner. Dolan, paranoid and gloating, announces he'll "name a thief and a
  fraud among you tomorrow" and reveal a rewritten will. He insults nearly everyone. He
  locks the will inside the cryptex in his study.
- **10:00 PM** — Dolan retires to his study, agitated. **Buck** brings the nightly
  buttermilk, sets it on the desk, leaves. (At this point it is **clean**.)
- **10:15–10:50 PM** — Guests scatter:
  - **Antoninski + Laurel** slip out the side door into the woods to stage a fresh
    sasquatch "sighting" for the Contessa. (Together, away from the house, until ~11:30.)
  - **Contessa** retires upstairs, in and out of her room, fretting about money.
  - **Emma** is wasted in her room; **Ross** is with her, "tending" her (and rifling her
    purse). They alibi each other.
  - **Dave** is on a long-distance call to Atlanta, on the manor's only phone line, ~10:30
    to ~11:20 (operator log will confirm). This is why he **can't** vouch for Gwen's
    whereabouts.
  - **Christopher Walken** holds court in the parlor, performing monologues to a rotating
    audience (Contessa, Buck, servants) — well-witnessed all evening.
  - **Birdy** heads down to the docks to secure her boat against the storm.
- **10:50 PM** — **Gwen** tells Dave she has a headache and goes "up to bed." Instead she
  slips into Dolan's study, carrying her art-restoration kit. She means to make peace; he
  instead confronts her with the forged-painting proof from his ledger. She stirs **Paris
  Green** into his buttermilk while soothing him.
- **11:00 PM** — Dolan drinks. Gwen leaves. In her haste she **drops a peach-colored
  monogrammed glove** ("G.B.") near the study door and tracks a faint **emerald-green
  smudge** of pigment onto the desk and glass.
- **11:00 PM** — **Birdy**, back from the docks for her forgotten keys, **glimpses Gwen
  hurrying out of the study wing** — not upstairs, where Gwen later claims she was.
- **11:15 PM** — Dolan dies at his desk, slumped over the cryptex.
- **Overnight** — Storm hits; bridge and phones go out. Everyone stays.
- **~7:00 AM (game start)** — **Buck** finds the body, locks the study, and gathers the
  stranded guests. The will is sealed in the cryptex; nobody can leave; the game begins.

**Physical-consistency check:** At 10:50–11:15 the only people *without* a corroborated
location are **Gwen** (claims "in bed," actually in the study) and **Birdy** (at the
docks, then briefly in the hall — she's the eyewitness). Everyone else is paired or
publicly witnessed. The killer's window is clean and exclusive to Gwen.

## 6. Solution clue-chain

Five key clues. Together they point **uniquely** to Gwen. Each is planted with redundancy
(at least two sources) so the solution can't die if one player clams up.

1. **CAUSE OF DEATH = arsenical artist's pigment (Paris Green).** Not moonshine, not lab
   chemicals, not "natural causes." A poison made of *emerald-green paint pigment* points
   to someone with **art-restoration materials**. *Sources:* Buck's examination of the
   body/glass (R2); confirmed by the narrator's "coroner's note" (R2). *Rules out:* anyone
   without artist's pigments — i.e., everyone but Gwen.

2. **THE EMERALD SMUDGE & THE GLOVE.** A faint green pigment smudge on the desk and glass,
   plus a **peach-colored monogrammed glove ("G.B.")** found by the study door. *Sources:*
   Buck found and bagged the glove (R3); Birdy noticed "something peach-colored" dropped in
   the hall (R3). The monogram and pigment tie the scene to Gwen.

3. **THE FORGERY (motive).** Dolan's prize painting in the study is a **fake**, and his
   will/ledger reveal he'd "uncovered a forger among the art-loving guests" and meant to
   name them publicly. *Sources:* the will, read after the cryptex opens (R1); Dolan's
   ledger, which Buck holds and reveals (R2–R3). Only **Gwen** had both the access and the
   art expertise to swap originals for fakes.

4. **THE BROKEN ALIBI (opportunity).** Gwen claims she was "in bed with a headache" from
   ~10:50. But **Birdy saw her leaving the study wing at 11:00**, and **Dave was on the
   phone** the whole time and *cannot* confirm she was upstairs. *Sources:* Birdy's
   eyewitness account (R3); Dave's admission about the phone call (R2–R3).

5. **THE KIT.** Gwen travels with an **art-restoration kit containing Paris Green**. When
   Buck (ex-detective) searches, the kit's pigment **matches** the smudge on the glass.
   *Sources:* Buck's search/reveal (R4); Gwen can be cornered into admitting she "restores"
   paintings (R3).

**Intended deduction (one breath):** The poison is an arsenical *paint* pigment (clue 1),
traced to the scene by a green smudge and Gwen's monogrammed glove (clue 2); Gwen had the
unique motive because Dolan had discovered *she* was forging his art and was about to
expose her (clue 3); she lied about being in bed and was actually seen leaving the study
at the time of death (clue 4); and her own restoration kit holds the exact pigment that
killed him (clue 5). Means, motive, and opportunity converge on **Gwen Buchthal** and no
one else — every other suspect either lacked the pigment, had a corroborated alibi, or
needed Dolan *alive*.

## 8. Round plan

- **R1 — The Gathering & the Cryptex.** Open on the body. Storm strands everyone. The will
  is locked in Dolan's cryptex; the guests must **pool ten clues to spell the 6-letter
  word and open it** (see §10). The will plants the forgery motive and casts suspicion
  wide. Dramatic purpose: meet everyone, establish that *everybody wanted something* from
  Dolan, force collaboration.
- **R2 — The Body Speaks.** The coroner's note reveals **poison by arsenical pigment**.
  Buck produces the forgery ledger. Suspicion swings toward art-and-money people and toward
  Antoninski (who's sweating). Dramatic purpose: it's murder, and it's about the art.
- **R3 — The Study Gives Up Its Secrets.** A search yields the **glove and the green
  smudge**; **Birdy's eyewitness account** breaks Gwen's alibi; the woods alibi clears
  Antoninski and exposes the fake sasquatch; Dave/Emma/Ross alibis resolve. Dramatic
  purpose: the noose tightens on Gwen while red herrings fall away.
- **R4 — Accusation & Reveal.** Vote, then Buck produces **Gwen's pigment kit**. Walk the
  clue-chain; reveal Gwen; resolve every red herring. Dramatic purpose: payoff.

## 9. The victim / ghost-helper role (Dr. Dolan)

Dolan is **dead at start**, so his player isn't benched — recast them as the **ghost of
Dr. Dolan / the host's deputy**. Give them the short ghost handout (`characters/
dr-connor-dolan.md`): they may not speak as a living character or reveal the solution, but
they hover, haunt theatrically, and **quietly help the host watch for lies** (flagging
when a player's claim contradicts the timeline in §4). If you're short a body to host, the
Dolan player can also read narrator scripts. Optionally they may "manifest" for dramatic
effect during the R4 reveal.

## 10. The Cryptex Puzzle (Round 1 mechanic)

**The answer word: `CRABBY`** (6 letters) — Dolan's temperament *and* a nod to his beloved
crab-people. The cryptex (a 6-ring lettered cylinder, or a 6-box combination/printout the
host controls) opens to reveal the will only when set to **C-R-A-B-B-Y**.

Ten guests, ten clue fragments. **Six** guests each hold a riddle yielding one **letter +
its position**; **four** guests hold **meta-clues** that confirm the word's shape and
meaning. All ten are needed to solve it confidently and fairly.

**Letter clues (position → letter):**
1. **Christopher Walken** → *"The first letter is... the third star... in the alphabet's
   parade. Wow."* → **position 1 = C**
2. **Ross Coken** → *"Second verse, same as the... well, no. The 18th letter. The one that
   rolls off a Scotsman's tongue, aye — rrrr."* → **position 2 = R**
3. **Dr. Antoninski** → *"The third is the first. The alpha. In Soviet alphabet, it is also
   first: A."* → **position 3 = A**
4. **Birdy Robbins** → *"Fourth slot. It's the letter that buoys a boat and starts my own
   name, Birdy. A big ol' B."* → **position 4 = B**
5. **Buck Shanty** → *"Position five, doll. Same letter as position four — history repeats.
   B, like Buck, like Baltimore."* → **position 5 = B** (and signals the repeat)
6. **Laurel of the Woods** → *"Last one, sugar — the tail end. The letter that asks a
   question and ends 'em too: Y. Why? Y."* → **position 6 = Y**

**Meta-clues (confirmation / decoding):**
7. **Gwen Buchthal** → *"The word is six letters long — no more, no less, my peaches."*
   (confirms length)
8. **Dave Buchthal** → *"One letter shows up twice in this here word. Sweet sassafras,
   it's a repeater!"* (confirms the double letter)
9. **Contessa Savoca** → *"The word describes the late doctor's charming temperament — and
   his precious little sea-creatures. Tutti crabby, no?"* (confirms the meaning/theme)
10. **Emma Lucas** → *"I had this, like, totally cosmic vision, man... the answer starts
    hard and ends with a question mark of a letter. C... to... Y. Far out."* (confirms
    first & last letters / sanity check)

**Host mechanic:** Hand each player their fragment on a slip (or printed at the bottom of
their R1 handout). Players read clues aloud and assemble **C-R-A-B-B-Y**. When they call
it, the host "opens" the cryptex and reads the will (R1 narrator, §below in round files).
If the table stalls, the host can let them dial guesses, or have the Dolan-ghost drop a
hint. **No murder clue is gated behind the cryptex** — if they brute-force it, the story
still works; the puzzle is a collaboration warm-up.

> Print note: if no physical cryptex, the host can use a 6-slot printout, a padlock with a
> letter dial, or simply require the spoken word `CRABBY` before reading the will.

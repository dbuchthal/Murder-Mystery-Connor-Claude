# Murder at Hot Shot Manor — Story Bible

> **HOST ONLY — do not show to players.** This is the complete ground truth. Every
> character background, round handout, and narrator script is a *view* onto this file.
> If a fact isn't here, it isn't true.
>
> **Design rules enforced in this version (read before running):**
> 1. **Strict fog-of-war.** No player document tells a character anything that character
>    doesn't already know. Character backgrounds have **no "What you don't know" section.**
>    A secret belonging to character X is never written on character Y's sheet *until the
>    round after X has revealed it in play.*
> 2. **Secrets are surfaced by OTHER players.** Every secret in a background is **questioned
>    by at least two different characters** (each given a legitimate observational *hook*,
>    never the secret itself) and is **revealed by the secret-holder** when questioned.
> 3. **The murder method is concealed.** Before Round 4, the poison is referred to **only**
>    as "a green poisonous substance" found in the buttermilk. The term **"Paris Green"**
>    (and "arsenical pigment") appears **only in Round 4.**
> 4. **Multiple poison suspects.** Four characters have green-poison access — **Kristof**
>    (mycologist; poisonous mushrooms), **Laurel** (laces moonshine with green liqueurs),
>    **Birdy** (toxic green kelp off her anchor chain), and the killer **Gwen** (arsenical
>    emerald paint pigment, concealed until R4). Their poison skills are revealed in play.

---

## 1. Premise & setting

**Hot Shot Manor, La Conner, Washington. The dead of night, autumn 1989.** Rain is
threatening but hasn't broken — yet. The manor belongs to **Dr. Connor Dolan**,
world-renowned cryptozoologist, eccentric trillionaire-of-the-tide-pools, and the only
man alive who claims to have shaken hands (claws?) with the aquatic **crab-people of the
San Juan Islands**.

Dolan summoned a houseful of glittering, grasping guests for a weekend "announcement
gala" — he promised to reveal something that would "rearrange the furniture of several
lives." Then, before he could say a word, he turned up **dead at his desk**, slumped over
his beloved **cryptex safe**, the one holding his freshly rewritten will. A coastal storm
has knocked out the bridge and the phones. Nobody leaves. Nobody calls the cops.

Somebody at this party is a killer. Everybody at this party is a liar.

**Tone:** play it like a 1920s film-noir potboiler that fell through a wormhole into
1989 — smoke-machine atmosphere and hard-boiled narration, but stuffed with Reagan-era
slang, neon, synthesizers, VHS tapes, and shoulder pads. Campy, absurdist, knowing. The
narrator is a gravel-voiced gumshoe who keeps getting distracted by the decade.

## 2. Cast

> Each entry lists the character's own knowledge. The **poison-access** line (where present)
> and **private secret** are *host-side facts that the player knows about themselves* — but
> they only **admit** them when another player questions them (see §7b).

### Dr. Connor Dolan — "Dead Man Walking" — THE VICTIM
- **Role:** The host. Cryptozoologist; obsessed with contacting the crab-people. Old,
  ailing, paranoid, fabulously rich.
- **Public persona:** A genius gone to seed. Recently convinced everyone was stealing
  from him — and, as it turns out, several of them were.
- **Private secret:** He had **proof of an art forgery ring** operating right under his
  nose. His private ledger documented that pieces of his celebrated art collection had
  been quietly swapped for fakes. He'd scheduled a press conference to name the thief and
  was rewriting his entire estate to punish the people who'd been bleeding him.
- **Relationships:** Patron, mark, or obstacle to nearly everyone here.
- **Voice:** Querulous, grandiose, paranoid. Dead, so relevant as backstory and the
  ghost-helper role (see §9).

### Gwen Buchthal — "The Georgia Peach" — **THE KILLER**
- **Role:** Atlanta transplant, rising star of Seattle high society, board member of the
  Seattle Art Museum.
- **Public persona:** All Southern charm and biting wit. Petitioning Dolan to donate prize
  pieces of his collection to the museum. Catchphrase: *"My peaches, what a sight!"*
- **Poison access (concealed until R4):** Gwen is a trained art restorer and travels with a
  **restoration kit containing Paris Green** — an emerald, arsenical oil-paint pigment. This
  is the murder weapon. **Do not let the words "Paris Green" or "pigment-as-poison" reach the
  table before R4.**
- **Private secret (THE MURDER):** Gwen is the forger. For two years she has quietly sold
  Dolan's genuine masterpieces on the black market and hung flawless fakes in their place,
  bankrolling her social climb. Dolan **discovered the swap** and confronted her last night,
  threatening to name her at tomorrow's press conference. She killed him by stirring **Paris
  Green** into his nightly buttermilk.
- **Relationships:** Married to Dave (who knows nothing of the forgery). Publicly fawning
  over Dolan; privately terrified of him.
- **Voice:** Honeyed Georgia drawl, lethal under the sugar. Deflects with charm.

### Dave Buchthal — "The Big Daddy"
- **Role:** Coca-Cola VP from Atlanta, in town to open a Seattle bottling plant.
- **Public persona:** Cigar-chomping dealmaker negotiating to buy a La Conner land parcel
  from Dolan. Catchphrase: *"Sweet sassafras, I feel it deep in my plums!"*
- **Private secret:** The Seattle plant is a **financial disaster** and Dave has been
  **cooking the books**, moving Coca-Cola relocation funds to cover his losses. He
  desperately needed Dolan to sign the land deal — it was the only thing that could've made
  the numbers work. **He needed Dolan alive.**
- **Relationships:** Gwen's devoted husband; genuinely doesn't know she's the forger or the
  killer.
- **Voice:** Booming, folksy, sweaty bravado.

### Emma Lucas — "The Uke of Hazard"
- **Role:** World-famous 1980s ukulele pop star, mid-pivot to the "electric synth-uke."
- **Public persona:** Dolan's favorite musician; a burned-out icon on a "journey of
  self-discovery."
- **Private secret:** She's **broke.** The synth-uke flopped and she came to the gala to
  **beg Dolan for a loan.** She was genuinely wasted last night.
- **Relationships:** Adored by Dolan; tended (and quietly fleeced) by Ross. She has begun to
  *suspect* her money has been going missing, but doesn't know Ross is the cause.
- **Voice:** Spacey, grandiose, drifts off mid-sentence.

### Ross Coken — "Remora Ross"
- **Role:** Scottish ex-carnie contortionist; the world's most famous rock groupie.
- **Public persona:** Charming hanger-on attached to Emma.
- **Private secret:** Now the tour's on hiatus and Emma's broke, Ross has been **skimming
  from Emma's accounts** for months and was shopping for his next "host" — possibly Dolan.
- **Relationships:** Latched onto Emma; alibis her for last night (they were together).
- **Voice:** Thick Scottish patter, carnival-barker flourishes.

### Christopher Walken — "Walken on Sunshine"
- **Role:** A-list actor, fashion icon, dancer; key investor in Dolan's research.
- **Public persona:** Loves reminding people he "used to be kind of a big deal" in Seattle
  fashion. Distinctive... halting... speech. A genuine **art-and-fashion connoisseur**.
- **Private secret:** "Walken" is a stage name — he's really **Christopher Chappman** — and
  his finances are collapsing. He **secretly withdrew his investment** in Dolan's research
  weeks ago and dreads it coming out.
- **Relationships:** Celebrity friend of Dolan; performed in the parlor all evening
  (well-witnessed). His connoisseur's eye also half-noticed something *off* about Dolan's
  paintings (a hook against Gwen).
- **Voice:** The Walken cadence. Strange. Pauses. Where you... wouldn't. Expect them. Wow.

### Ms. Jay "Birdy" Robbins — "The Canary in the Coal Mine"
- **Role:** Owner of the Red Irish Lord fishery; Dolan's offshore research contractor.
- **Public persona:** Salt-crusted boat captain funded by Dolan's grants.
- **Poison access:** Birdy's anchor chain regularly drags up **toxic green kelp** (a real
  bloom hazard) that she has to handle and dispose of carefully — she knows exactly how
  poisonous the green slime is. A method red herring.
- **Private secret:** The "research vessels" are partly a **smuggling front**, and she's
  been **pocketing grant money** the IRS is now circling. An audit would sink her — so she
  **needed Dolan alive.**
- **Relationships:** Financially dependent on Dolan. Was at the docks last night — and,
  returning for keys, **saw Gwen leaving the study wing at 11pm** (the eyewitness).
- **Voice:** Gruff, nautical, deadpan.

### Dr. Kristof Antoninski — "The Severe Soviet"
- **Role:** Cryptozoologist from the University of Moscow; Dolan's "friendly rival."
  Front-runner for the 1989 Nobel for his sasquatch "discovery." Also a **trained
  mycologist.**
- **Public persona:** Severe, humorless, triumphant. The man who "found" the sasquatch.
- **Poison access:** As a mycologist he has deep knowledge of **poisonous mushrooms** and
  carries field specimens and extracts. A method red herring.
- **Private secret:** The sasquatch discovery is **completely faked** — costume, plaster
  footprints, the lot — built with Laurel's help, and **Dolan knew it** and was going to
  expose him. His whole Nobel rests on a lie. (Strong-looking motive — but see §5.)
- **Relationships:** Bitter rival of Dolan; employer of Laurel; secretly **bribed Contessa
  Savoca** for a favorable Nobel verdict (so he knows her secret).
- **Voice:** Clipped, grim, dramatic. "In Soviet cryptozoology..."

### Contessa Rebecca Savoca — "Italian Nobel-ity"
- **Role:** Head of the Nobel Committee on Cryptozoology; runs the Savoca Institute. Her
  verdict decides the prize.
- **Public persona:** Imperious noblewoman, final arbiter of scientific glory.
- **Private secret:** The Savoca Institute is **bankrupt**, and she has accepted a large
  **bribe from Antoninski** to rule in his favor. If either surfaces, she's ruined.
- **Relationships:** Compromised by Antoninski; drifted through the parlor (saw Walken
  perform) and overheard part of **Dave's frantic phone call** about money (a hook against
  Dave). She's also heard the high-society whisper that "Walken" is a broke fake (a hook
  against Walken).
- **Voice:** Haughty, continental, drips condescension.

### Laurel of the Woods — "The Moonshine Mama"
- **Role:** Backwoods moonshiner; hired by Antoninski as wilderness guide and tracker.
- **Public persona:** Folksy hill-country fixture with jugs of potent brew.
- **Poison access:** Laurel cuts her moonshine with homemade **green liqueurs** — wormwood
  and herb infusions potent enough to drop a moose. She knows her green brew can poison as
  easily as please. A method red herring.
- **Private secret:** She **built the fake sasquatch** for Antoninski's money and knows the
  whole fraud.
- **Relationships:** On Antoninski's payroll; **alibis him** for 11pm (they snuck into the
  woods to stage a sighting). She's seen Antoninski **foraging mushrooms** (a hook against
  him).
- **Voice:** Twangy, sly, folksy proverbs. Calls everyone "sugar" or "city-folk."

### Buck Shanty — "The Investigator"
- **Role:** Retired Baltimore private eye, now Dolan's personal valet.
- **Public persona:** Stone-faced manservant; last to see Dolan alive (delivered the
  nightcap).
- **Private secret:** Dolan secretly hired Buck **to investigate the guests.** Buck keeps a
  notebook of **leads and questions** — not full confessions, but enough to *press* people.
  He is the table's **engine of pressure**, not an oracle: he prompts; the holders confess.
- **Relationships:** Loyal to Dolan; quietly investigating everyone. His one vanity: proving
  the old Baltimore instincts haven't gone soft.
- **Voice:** Clipped hard-boiled Baltimore PI. "The dame had a story. They always do."

## 3. The crime

- **Victim:** Dr. Connor Dolan.
- **Method:** Poisoning.
- **Weapon/means (HOST-ONLY until R4):** **Paris Green** — emerald, arsenical oil-paint
  pigment from Gwen's art-restoration kit — stirred into Dolan's nightly **buttermilk.**
  **Before R4 this is described to players only as "a green poisonous substance."**
- **Location:** Dolan's private study, at the desk where the cryptex safe sits.
- **Time of death:** ~11:15 PM (he drank the laced nightcap around 11:00 PM).
- **Killer:** Gwen Buchthal.
- **Motive:** Dolan discovered Gwen had been forging and selling his art collection and
  threatened to expose her at the next day's press conference. She silenced him.

## 4. True timeline (the previous night)

- **8:30 PM** — Guests assemble for the gala. Storm rolling in; the bridge will wash out.
- **9:00 PM** — Dinner. Dolan announces he'll "name a thief and a fraud among you tomorrow"
  and reveal a rewritten will; he insults nearly everyone. **Emma corners him, pleading for
  money** (witnessed at table). He locks the will in the study cryptex.
- **10:00 PM** — Dolan retires to his study. **Buck** brings the nightly buttermilk, sets it
  down, leaves. (At this point it is **clean.**)
- **10:15–10:50 PM** — Guests scatter:
  - **Antoninski + Laurel** slip out the side door into the woods to stage a fresh sasquatch
    "sighting" for the Contessa. (Together, away from the house, until ~11:30.) En route,
    Laurel sees Antoninski pocket **mushroom specimens**; they share a nip of her **green
    liqueur** brew.
  - **Contessa** retires upstairs, drifting through the parlor; she catches **Dave on the
    phone** in the hall nook, hissing about "the Atlanta numbers."
  - **Emma** is wasted in her room; **Ross** is with her (and rifling her purse). They alibi
    each other.
  - **Dave** is on a long-distance call to Atlanta on the manor's only line, ~10:30 to
    ~11:20 (operator log will confirm). He **can't** vouch for Gwen.
  - **Christopher Walken** holds court in the parlor, performing to a rotating audience
    (Contessa, Buck, staff) — well-witnessed all evening.
  - **Birdy** heads to the docks to secure her boat against the storm.
- **10:50 PM** — **Gwen** tells Dave she has a headache and goes "up to bed." Instead she
  slips into Dolan's study with her restoration kit. He confronts her with the forged-painting
  proof. She stirs **Paris Green** into his buttermilk while soothing him.
- **11:00 PM** — Dolan drinks. Gwen leaves, dropping a **peach-colored monogrammed glove
  ("G.B.")** near the door and leaving a faint **greenish smear** on the desk and glass.
- **11:00 PM** — **Birdy**, back for her forgotten keys, **glimpses Gwen hurrying out of the
  study wing** — not upstairs, where Gwen later claims she was.
- **11:15 PM** — Dolan dies at his desk, slumped over the cryptex.
- **Overnight** — Storm hits; bridge and phones go out. Everyone stays.
- **~7:00 AM (game start)** — **Buck** finds the body, locks the study, gathers the guests.

**Physical-consistency check:** During 10:50–11:15 the only people *without* a corroborated
location are **Gwen** (claims "in bed," actually in the study) and **Birdy** (at the docks,
then briefly in the hall — the eyewitness). Everyone else is paired or publicly witnessed.
The killer's window is clean and exclusive to Gwen.

## 6. Solution clue-chain

Five key clues. Together they point **uniquely** to Gwen, with redundancy so the solution
survives a quiet player. **Note the deliberately *delayed* identification of the weapon.**

1. **A GREEN POISON (R2) — ambiguous on purpose.** The coroner's note says only that a
   **green poisonous substance** was in the buttermilk. This implicates *anyone with green
   poison*: Antoninski (mushroom extracts), Laurel (green liqueur), Birdy (toxic kelp), and —
   unknown to the table — Gwen (paint pigment). *Sources:* narrator coroner's note (R2);
   Buck confirms the nightcap was clean at 10:00 (poison added after). This widens suspicion;
   it does **not** by itself name the killer.
2. **THE GREENISH SMEAR & THE GLOVE (R3).** A faint **greenish smear** on the desk and glass,
   plus a **peach-colored monogrammed glove ("G.B.")** by the study door. *Sources:* Buck
   bags the glove; Birdy noticed "something peach-colored" dropped. Ties the scene to Gwen.
3. **THE FORGERY = MOTIVE (R1 will → R2 ledger).** Dolan's will/ledger reveal he'd "uncovered
   a forger among the art-loving guests" and meant to name them. Only **Gwen** had the access
   and the art skill. *Sources:* the will (R1); Buck's ledger (R2); Walken's connoisseur
   observation that a painting looked *wrong* (R3).
4. **THE BROKEN ALIBI = OPPORTUNITY (R3).** Gwen claims she was "in bed." But **Birdy saw her
   leaving the study wing at 11:00**, and **Dave was on the phone** the whole time and cannot
   confirm she was upstairs. *Sources:* Birdy (R3); Dave (R3). This *also* clears the other
   poison suspects, who were elsewhere (woods/docks/parlor/room) with corroboration.
5. **THE IDENTIFICATION + THE KIT (R4).** The green substance is finally identified as
   **Paris Green** — an arsenical *oil-paint pigment* — matching the greenish smear, and
   **not** kelp, mushroom, or liqueur. Buck's search turns up **Gwen's restoration kit
   containing the same Paris Green.** *Sources:* narrator + Buck (R4).

**Intended deduction:** The poison was green (clue 1) — so at first *four* people look
capable. But three of them (Antoninski, Laurel, Birdy) have **corroborated alibis** for the
murder window (clue 4), and the green at the scene was a dry **smear/pigment**, not a brewed
liquid — pointing to *paint,* identified in R4 as Paris Green (clues 2, 5). The only suspect
with paint access, no alibi, a monogrammed glove at the scene, an eyewitness against her, and
a motive (Dolan was about to expose her forgery — clue 3) is **Gwen Buchthal.**

is **revealed by the holder** when questioned. Questioner handouts carry the *hook*; holder

## 8. Round plan

- **R1 — The Gathering & the Cryptex.** Open on the body. Pool ten clues to spell `CRABBY`
  and open the cryptex; read the will (forgery motive seeded; suspicion wide). Meet everyone.
- **R2 — The Body Speaks.** Coroner's note: a **green poisonous substance.** Suddenly the
  three openly poison-skilled guests (Antoninski/Laurel/Birdy) look dangerous; Buck shows the
  forgery ledger. Dramatic purpose: it's murder, the weapon is *green,* and several people
  know green poisons.
- **R3 — The Study Gives Up Its Secrets.** The **glove + greenish smear**; **Birdy's
  eyewitness** breaks Gwen's alibi; the woods alibi clears Antoninski & exposes the hoax;
  financial/identity secrets fall. Dramatic purpose: alibis clear the poison red herrings and
  the noose tightens on Gwen.
- **R4 — Accusation & Reveal.** Vote; identify the substance as **Paris Green**; produce
  Gwen's matching kit; walk the chain; resolve every red herring.

## 9. The victim / ghost-helper role (Dr. Dolan)

Dolan is **dead at start**, so his player becomes the **ghost of Dr. Dolan / host's deputy**
(see `characters/dr-connor-dolan.md`): they may not speak as a living character or reveal the
solution, but they haunt, hint atmospherically, and **quietly help the host watch for lies**
against the §4 timeline. They may read narrator scripts and "manifest" for the R4 reveal.

## 10. The Cryptex Puzzle (Round 1 mechanic)

**Answer word: `CRABBY`** (Dolan's temperament *and* his crab-people). The cryptex (a 6-ring
cylinder, a letter-dial padlock, or a host-controlled 6-slot printout) opens to the will only
when set to **C-R-A-B-B-Y.**

Ten guests, ten fragments. **Six** hold a riddle giving one **letter + position**; **four**
hold **meta-clues** confirming the word's shape and meaning. All ten are needed to solve it
fairly.

**Letter clues (position → letter):**
1. **Christopher Walken** → *"The first letter is... the third star... in the alphabet's
   parade. Wow."* → **position 1 = C**
2. **Ross Coken** → *"Second slot — the 18th letter, the one that rolls off a Scotsman's
   tongue: rrrr."* → **position 2 = R**
3. **Dr. Antoninski** → *"The third is the first. The alpha. In Soviet alphabet, also first:
   A."* → **position 3 = A**
4. **Birdy Robbins** → *"Fourth slot — the letter that buoys a boat and starts my own name,
   Birdy. A big ol' B."* → **position 4 = B**
5. **Buck Shanty** → *"Position five, doll — same letter as position four. History repeats.
   B, like Buck, like Baltimore."* → **position 5 = B** (signals the repeat)

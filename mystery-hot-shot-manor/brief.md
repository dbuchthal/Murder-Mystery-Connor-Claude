# Murder Mystery Brief

## Setting
Hot Shot Manor in La Connor, Washington. The year is 1989. Rain is threatening but has not yet begun.

## Tone & voice
The tone of this should be like a 1920s film noir, but set in an 1980s setting. It should use 1980s slang, lingo and cultural references. The dialog should be campy and absurdist.


## Rounds
4. (R1: the gathering and unlocking the safe. R2 and R3: the investigation. R4: accusation and reveal.)



## Players
- Gwen Buchthal, aka "The Georgia Peach". Quickly ascending to the dizzying pinnacle of Seattle high society, Gwen Buchthal is a recent transplant from Atlanta, along with her devoted husband. An enthusiastic art connoisseur on the board of the Seattle Art Museum, she has been petitioning Dr. Dolan to donate several pieces of his prized collection. With biting wit and Southern charm, her iconic catchphrase is “My peaches, what a sight!”
- Dave Bucthhal, aka "The Big Daddy". “Big Daddy Dave” is a high-powered Vice President for the Coca-Cola corporation in Atlanta. Tasked with opening a new Seattle bottling plant, the Buchthals relocated to the Evergreen State and Big Daddy was negotiating with Dr. Dolan to purchase a large plot of land in La Conner. Frequently seen chomping on a cigar as he wheels and deals, his iconic catchphrase is “Sweet sassafras, I feel it deep in my plums!”
- Emma Lucas, aka "The Uke of Hazard". The world-famous ukelele musician, Emma spent her youth touring the world, “wailing on the uke”, and leaving a trail of broken hearts in her wake. Recently, this 80s pop star announced a pivot to the electric synth-uke, spawning a great deal of controversy in musical circles. Her legendary band (Dr. Dolan’s favorite), “The Emma Lucas Experience”, recently announced a hiatus to their international tour as Emma embarked on a drug-fueled journey of self-discovery.
- Ross Coken, aka "Remora Ross". Just like the humble remora latches onto a larger shark to feed off their scraps… groupies latch onto rock stars to feed off their fame. As Emma Lucas rose to prominence, she attracted Ross Coken: infamous Scottish party-boy, lover, and the most famous rock groupie on earth. Ross previously toured Scotland as a carnie and human contortionist until he decided that he would rather bend over backwards for Emma, catering to her every need. Ross follows the band around the world, providing companionship, domestic assistance, and serving as a creative muse. At least… until the recent tour hiatus.
- Christopher Walken, aka "Walken on Sunshine". Famous A-list Hollywood actor, fashion icon, and professional dancer Christopher Walken is a close celebrity friend and key investor in Dr. Dolan’s research. Few realize that “Walken” is a stage name, and his original boring surname was “Chappman”. He enjoys reminding people that he used to be “kind of a big deal” in Seattle fashion. Christopher Walken is noted for his consistently distinctive style of speech.
- Ms. Jay "Birdy" Robbins, aka "The Canary in the Coal Mine". The owner of the Red Irish Lord fishery in the San Juan islands, where Dr. Dolan conducted much of his research looking for the mysterious and elusive crab-people. As his health declined, Dr. Dolan became more reliant on Birdy to conduct expeditions offshore. His latest IRS filings indicated that a significant portion of Dr. Dolan’s grant money was being funneled to Birdy Robbins and her fleet of fishing vessels repurposed into maritime research.
- Dr. Kristof Antoninski, aka "The Severe Soviet". A fellow cryptozoologist from the University of Moscow and a “friendly rival” to Dr. Dolan. Dr. Antoniski relocated to the Pacific Northwest to begin his enthusiastic pursuit of the elusive sasquatch, the gentle forest ape. This was a (reportedly) successful endeavor which launched him to superstardom within the cryptozoology community and has resulted in his expected nomination for the coveted 1989 Nobel Prize.
- Contessa Rebecca Savoca, aka "Italian Nobel-ity". Contessa Rebecca Savoca is the head of the Nobel Committee on Cryptozoology, and leader of the Savoca Institute of Science in Palermo, Sicily. The orphaned daughter of a diminished noble family and inheritor to Castle Savoca, the Contessa inherited her family’s remaining wealth at a young age, which she eventually transformed into a world-class scientific institute. Her personal evaluation of the competition between Dr. Dolan and Dr. Antoniski will likely be the determining factor in which scientist takes home the Nobel Prize.
- Laurel of the Woods, aka "The Moonshine Mama". The deep forests of the Pacific Northwest are home to many a hillbilly moonshiner, and Laurel of the Woods is one such local fixture. With her big jugs of potent brew, the “Moonshine Mama” was a welcome sight in any back country bar or roadhouse. Her intimate knowledge of the woods caught the attention of Dr. Antoniski, who hired her a guide and tracker for the near-mythical sasquatch.
- Buck Shanty, aka “The Investigator”. Buck Shanty is a hard-knuckled retired private detective from the gritty streets of Baltimore, currently serving as the personal valet to Dr Dolan.
- Dr. Connor Dolan, aka "Dead Man Walking". Dr. Connor Dolan is a world-renown cryptozoologist. Eccentric and increasingly paranoid in recent years as he was beset by various health issues, Dr. Dolan has staked much of his vast wealth and reputation on his frenzied quest to contact the aquatic crab-people of the San Juan Islands.

## The victim
Conor Dolan


## Victim mode
dead at start


## The killer
Gwen Buchthal


## Anything else
In round 1, the goal is to open the safe so that the players can see the will. The safe is contained in a Cryptex puzzle that is opened with a particular 6-letter word that the players must collaborate to reveal. Each player should have a clue that is required to complete the puzzle.

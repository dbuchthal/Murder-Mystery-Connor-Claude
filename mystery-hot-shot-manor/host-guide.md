# Host Guide — Murder at Hot Shot Manor

Everything you need to run the night. Practical, not in-character. **You and the Dolan-ghost
player are the only people who should read `bible.md`, this guide, and the narrator scripts.**

## Overview
- **Premise:** Cryptozoologist Dr. Connor Dolan is found poisoned at his desk, slumped over the
  cryptex safe holding his rewritten will. A storm strands the guests; no police are coming. The
  guests must open the cryptex, investigate, and unmask the killer.
- **Setting & tone:** Hot Shot Manor, La Conner, WA, 1989 — a 1920s film-noir whodunit refracted
  through neon-and-synth 1980s camp. Encourage big accents and bigger shoulder pads.
- **Players:** 10 living suspects + 1 victim (Dr. Dolan, dead at start → plays the **ghost /
  host's deputy**). The killer is **Gwen Buchthal** — known only to you and Gwen's player.
- **Rounds:** 4. **Runtime:** ~2 to 2.5 hours (R1 ~30 min · R2 ~30 · R3 ~35 · R4 ~40).

## How this kit works (read this — it's unusual)
This is a **"everyone is guilty-looking"** mystery solved by a single fused deduction:
- **Strict fog-of-war.** No player sheet tells a character anything they don't already know.
  Backgrounds have no "what you don't know" section; a secret never appears on another player's
  sheet until the round *after* it's revealed in play.
- **Secrets surface because *other players* ask.** Every secret has **≥2** characters primed with
  a legitimate *hook* (something they witnessed/know — never the secret itself); the **holder**
  confesses. Steer the questioners using their "Threads you can pull" sections. Full map:
  **bible.md §7c**.
- **EVERYONE looks guilty.** By the end of Round 3 every guest has revealed (a) **access to a
  poison**, (b) **a fraud/forgery connection**, (c) a **motive**, and (d) a **murky alibi**. There
  is **no clean alibi for anyone** and **no single clue** (no glove, no naming eyewitness) pointing
  at the killer. *This is intentional — don't let the table "solve" it in R3.*
- **The only solution is the art × poison FUSION (R4).** The poison is identified as **Paris
  Green — an oil-paint pigment**, and the ledger shows the forger **repainted Dolan's canvases.**
  Among ten poisoners and ten fakers, only **Gwen** both *forges paintings* and *works in paint.*
  The murder weapon is literally her forger's medium. No other path reaches her.
- **The method is hidden until R4.** Through R2–R3 the poison is "a green poisonous substance."
  Do **not** say "Paris Green," "pigment," "arsenic," or "paint poison" before the R4 reveal.

## Before the party
**Print & hand to each player (their eyes only):** their **character background** from
`characters/<name>.md`.
**Keep host-only:** `bible.md`, this guide, and **every** `rounds/round-N/narrator.md`. Brief the
**Dolan-ghost player** from the bible.
**Print per round, handed out only at that round's start:** each `rounds/round-N/<name>.md`.

**Props (optional):** a **cryptex** / letter-dial padlock / 6-slot printout set to **`CRABBY`**;
the **will** (R1 narrator); a **"coroner's note"** (green residue, *unnamed*) and **forgery
ledger** for R2; a vial/swab of **green residue** (no glove!) for the R3 study search; a small
**art-restoration kit** with a jar labeled **"Paris Green"** for R4; a flashlight for the finale.

## The cryptex puzzle (Round 1)
Answer: **`CRABBY`.** Fragments: C(1) Walken · R(2) Ross · A(3) Antoninski · B(4) Birdy · B(5)
Buck · Y(6) Laurel; meta-clues from Gwen (length 6), Dave (a repeated letter), Contessa (meaning),
Emma (runs C…→…Y). Pool fragments, spell the word, read **The Will** at the foot of the R1
narrator. If brute-forced, just read the will and move on.

## Running each round
1. **Read the narrator script** (or let the Dolan-ghost perform it).
2. **Hand out** that round's per-character handouts.
3. **Let conversation run — and actively steer questioners to their targets.** If a confession
   isn't coming, prompt the player whose "Threads you can pull" points at it.
4. **Track the round's checklist** in the narrator host-notes.

**Round signposts:**
- **R1 — The Gathering & the Cryptex:** meet everyone; open the cryptex; read the will (the forger
  **repainted his canvases** — motive seeded).
- **R2 — Everybody's Got Poison:** coroner's note = a green poison; **every guest is outed as
  having access to a poison** (plus Laurel's stolen jug and lurkers at Birdy's boat, so anyone
  could've borrowed another's); Buck shows the ledger (painting forgery) and the clean nightcap.
- **R3 — Everybody's a Faker:** the study search finds **only green residue (no glove)**; **every
  guest is outed as some kind of fraud**; **alibis all collapse**; **Kristof is hard-pressed as
  the poisoner** (by Birdy & Walken); Walken confirms a canvas was painted over; Birdy reports an
  **unidentifiable figure.** *End state: every guest is a viable suspect.*
- **R4 — The Fusion:** vote; identify the poison as **Paris Green (oil paint)**; recall the
  **painting** forgery; show only Gwen sits in both circles; produce her kit; reveal; resolve the
  web of false leads.

## The finish (Round 4)
1. Each player **accuses** someone with one reason.
2. **Vote** (hands or secret ballot); tally aloud — it's for fun; the solution is fixed.
3. **Identify the poison as Paris Green** and **recall the painting forgery**; **walk the fusion**:
   only Gwen forges paintings *and* works in paint. **Produce her kit.**
4. **Gwen confesses** (campy villain monologue encouraged).
5. **Resolve every false lead** with the wrap so each guest learns why they *almost* fit but sat
   in only one circle.
6. Optional **awards** from the Dolan-ghost.

## Troubleshooting
- **A player is absent:** Easiest cuts are Ross (fold his cons into Emma's story) or the Contessa
  (reassign her hooks against Dave/Walken to Buck). **Never cut Gwen or Buck** (killer,
  detective). Avoid cutting Antoninski/Laurel singly (they alibi each other and anchor the R3
  Kristof-as-poisoner beat). Reassign any missing key role to a confident player or the host.
- **A confession won't come out:** every secret has two primed questioners plus Buck — prompt
  whichever is present (their "Threads you can pull" names the target).
- **The table "solves" it in R3:** gently resist — point out that the suspect they've fixed on had
  a poison and a fraud, *but so does everyone,* and nothing yet proves *which* poison or *which*
  kind of forgery. Keep all ten in the frame until the R4 fusion.
- **Keep the method secret:** do not let "Paris Green," "pigment," "arsenic," or "paint poison" be
  said before R4. In R2–R3 the honest answer is "it's green and deadly — and half this room has a
  green poison."
- **Why there's no glove:** by request, the only path to the killer is the art × poison fusion.
  Don't reintroduce scene clues that shortcut it.

# Host Guide — Murder at Hot Shot Manor

Everything you need to run the night. Practical, not in-character. **You and the Dolan-ghost
player are the only people who should read `bible.md`, this guide, and the narrator scripts.**

## Overview
- **Premise:** Cryptozoologist Dr. Connor Dolan is found poisoned at his desk, slumped over the
  cryptex safe holding his rewritten will. A storm strands the guests; no police are coming.
  The guests must open the cryptex, investigate, and unmask the killer.
- **Setting & tone:** Hot Shot Manor, La Conner, WA, 1989 — a 1920s film-noir whodunit
  refracted through neon-and-synth 1980s camp. Encourage big accents and bigger shoulder pads.
- **Players:** 10 living suspects + 1 victim (Dr. Dolan, dead at start → plays the **ghost /
  host's deputy**). The killer is **Gwen Buchthal** — known only to you and Gwen's player.
- **Rounds:** 4. **Runtime:** ~2 to 2.5 hours (R1 ~30 min · R2 ~25 · R3 ~30 · R4 ~35).

## How this kit is built (read this — it changes how you run it)
This version runs on **strict fog-of-war** and **player-driven reveals**:
- **No player sheet tells a character anything they don't already know.** Backgrounds have no
  "what you don't know" section, and a character's secret never appears on anyone else's sheet
  until *after* it's been revealed in play.
- **Secrets come out because *other players* ask the right questions.** Every secret has **two
  or more** characters primed with a legitimate *hook* (something they witnessed or know) that
  leads them to question the secret-holder. The **holder** then confesses. Your job is to make
  sure those questions actually get asked — nudge the questioners using their "Threads you can
  pull" sections. The full map is in **bible.md §7b**.
- **The murder weapon is hidden until Round 4.** Through Rounds 2–3 the poison is known only as
  **"a green poisonous substance."** Do **not** say "Paris Green," "pigment," or "arsenic"
  before the Round 4 reveal — those words are scripted into the R4 narrator only.
- **Four guests can make a green poison** (a deliberate web of red herrings): **Antoninski**
  (poisonous mushrooms), **Laurel** (green-liqueur moonshine), **Birdy** (toxic green kelp), and
  the killer **Gwen** (emerald paint pigment, concealed till R4). Three are cleared by alibis;
  the fourth is Gwen.

## Before the party
**Print and hand to each player (their eyes only):** their **character background** from
`characters/<name>.md` — a day ahead or on arrival.

**Keep host-only (do NOT print for players):** `bible.md`, this `host-guide.md`, and **every**
`rounds/round-N/narrator.md`. Brief the **Dolan-ghost player** from the bible so they can help
you watch for lies.

**Print per round, handed out only at the start of that round:** each player's
`rounds/round-N/<name>.md`. Never hand out future rounds early — the staged reveals depend on
it.

**Props (optional but great):** a **cryptex** (or letter-dial padlock / 6-slot printout) set to
**`CRABBY`**; the **will** (in the R1 narrator) on fancy paper; a **"coroner's note"** (green
residue, *unnamed*) and **forgery ledger** for R2; a **peach-colored glove** monogrammed "G.B."
and a green smear for R3; a small **art-restoration kit** with a jar labeled **"Paris Green"**
for R4; a flashlight for the stormy finale.

## The cryptex puzzle (Round 1)
Answer: **`CRABBY`.** Each R1 handout ends with one fragment — C(1) Walken · R(2) Ross · A(3)
Antoninski · B(4) Birdy · B(5) Buck · Y(6) Laurel, plus meta-clues from Gwen (length 6), Dave
(a repeated letter), Contessa (meaning), Emma (runs C…→…Y). Players pool fragments to spell the
word; then read **The Will** at the foot of the R1 narrator. If they brute-force it, just read
the will and move on — no murder clue is locked behind the puzzle.

## Running each round
1. **Read the narrator script** aloud (or let the Dolan-ghost perform it).
2. **Hand out** that round's per-character handouts.
3. **Let conversation run** — and actively **steer the questioners toward their targets.** If a
   secret isn't coming out, prompt the player whose "Threads you can pull" section points at it
   ("Contessa, didn't you overhear something on the phone last night?").
4. **Track the round's clue checklist** in the narrator host-notes; use the nudges if a beat
   stalls.
5. **Move on** when the round's clues and confessions are out.

**Round signposts:**
- **R1** — Meet everyone; open the cryptex; read the will (forgery motive seeded; suspicion
  wide).
- **R2** — Coroner's note: **a green poisonous substance.** The three openly poison-skilled
  guests (Antoninski/Laurel/Birdy) are questioned and admit their green-poison know-how; Buck
  shows the forgery ledger and the clean-nightcap timing. Suspicion *spreads.*
- **R3** — Study search yields the **glove + green smear**; **Birdy's eyewitness** + **Dave's
  phone call** break Gwen's alibi; the **woods alibi** clears Antoninski/Laurel (and exposes the
  fake sasquatch); the financial/identity secrets all fall (Dave, Emma, Ross, Walken, Birdy,
  Contessa); Buck reveals he's the investigator; Walken ties Gwen to the forged painting.
- **R4** — Accusations + vote; the poison is named **Paris Green** and matched to **Gwen's kit**;
  full reveal + clue-chain walk + red-herring wrap.

## The finish (Round 4)
1. Each player **accuses** someone with one reason.
2. **Vote** (hands or secret ballot); tally aloud — it's for fun; the solution is fixed.
3. **Identify the poison as Paris Green** and **produce Gwen's matching kit**; **walk the
   5-clue chain** (in the R4 narrator) so the answer is clearly **Gwen.**
4. **Gwen confesses** (campy villain monologue encouraged).
5. **Resolve every red herring** using the wrap so each innocent learns why they looked guilty.
6. Optional **awards** handed out by the Dolan-ghost.

## Troubleshooting
- **A player is absent:** Easiest cuts are Ross (fold his skimming into Emma's story) or the
  Contessa (her hooks against Dave/Walken can be reassigned to Buck). **Never cut Gwen, Buck, or
  Birdy** — killer, pressure-engine, eyewitness. Also avoid cutting the pair Antoninski/Laurel
  together (they alibi each other). Reassign a missing key role to a confident player or the
  host.
- **A secret won't come out:** every secret has **two** primed questioners plus Buck — prompt
  whichever is present (their "Threads you can pull" names the target). As a last resort, Buck
  presses with a notebook lead and the holder confesses.
- **The table stalls:** use the narrator host-note nudges, or have the Dolan-ghost drift and
  groan toward the relevant thread.
- **Someone guesses Gwen early:** fine — they still need the evidence. Withhold the Paris Green
  identification and the kit (R4) and let the chain play out.
- **Keep the method secret:** do not let "Paris Green," "pigment," or "arsenic" be said before
  R4. If asked what the green substance is, the honest answer in R2–R3 is "we don't know yet —
  only that it's green and deadly, and several people here know green poisons."

Have a blast. *My peaches — what a sight.*

# Host Guide — Murder at Hot Shot Manor

Everything you need to run the night. Practical, not in-character. **You and the Dolan-ghost
player are the only people who should read `bible.md`, this guide, and the narrator scripts.**

## Overview
- **Premise:** Cryptozoologist Dr. Connor Dolan is found poisoned at his desk, slumped over
  the cryptex safe holding his rewritten will. A storm strands the guests; no police are
  coming. The guests must open the cryptex, investigate, and unmask the killer.
- **Setting & tone:** Hot Shot Manor, La Conner, WA, 1989 — a 1920s film-noir whodunit
  refracted through neon-and-synth 1980s camp. Encourage big accents and bigger shoulder pads.
- **Players:** 10 living suspects + 1 victim (Dr. Dolan, dead at start → plays the **ghost /
  host's deputy**). The killer is **Gwen Buchthal** — known only to you and Gwen's player.
- **Rounds:** 4. **Runtime:** ~2 to 2.5 hours plus mingling (R1 ~30 min · R2 ~25 · R3 ~30 ·
  R4 ~35). Add a dinner break wherever it fits the 1980s vibe.

## Before the party
**Print and hand to each player (their eyes only):**
- Their **character background** from `characters/<name>.md` — give it a day ahead or on
  arrival so they can get into character.

**Keep host-only (do NOT print for players):**
- `bible.md`, this `host-guide.md`, and **every `rounds/round-N/narrator.md`**.
- Share `bible.md` (or a verbal briefing of the true timeline) with the **Dolan-ghost player**
  so they can help you spot lies.

**Print per round, to hand out at the start of that round only:**
- Each player's `rounds/round-N/<name>.md` handout. **Don't give future rounds early** — the
  staged reveals depend on it.

**Props (optional but great):**
- A **cryptex** (or letter-dial padlock, or a 6-slot printout) set to open on **`CRABBY`**.
- The **will** (in the R1 narrator script) printed on fancy paper, sealed in the cryptex.
- A **"coroner's note"** and **forgery ledger** (envelopes) for Buck/R2.
- A **peach-colored glove** monogrammed "G.B." and a smear of green (for R3).
- A small **art-restoration kit** with a jar labeled **"Paris Green"** (for R4).
- A flashlight for the stormy R4 lights-flicker.

## The cryptex puzzle (Round 1)
The answer is **`CRABBY`**. Each player's R1 handout ends with one clue fragment:
- **C**(1) Walken · **R**(2) Ross · **A**(3) Antoninski · **B**(4) Birdy · **B**(5) Buck ·
  **Y**(6) Laurel · plus meta-clues: length 6 (Gwen), a repeated letter (Dave), meaning =
  Dolan's temperament/crab-people (Contessa), runs C…→…Y (Emma).
Have players read fragments aloud and assemble the word. If stuck, let them guess on the dial
or have the ghost groan at correct letters. When solved, read **The Will** at the bottom of
the R1 narrator script. No murder clue is locked behind the puzzle — it's a collaboration
warm-up; if they brute-force it, just read the will and continue.

## Running each round
1. **Read the narrator script** aloud (or have the Dolan-ghost perform it). Set the scene and
   trigger the round's event.
2. **Hand out** that round's per-character handouts.
3. **Let conversation run** — players mingle, confront, trade clues. The "If pressed" sections
   are how clues pass between players; nudge people toward each other.
4. **Watch the clue checklist** in each narrator's host notes. If a required clue is stuck,
   use the listed nudges (or the ghost) to surface it.
5. **Move on** when the round's clues are out and energy peaks.

**Round signposts:** R1 = meet everyone + open the cryptex + read the will (motive seeded).
R2 = coroner's note reveals **Paris Green poison**; Buck shows the forgery ledger. R3 = study
search yields the **glove + green smudge**; **Birdy's eyewitness** breaks Gwen's alibi;
Antoninski/Laurel/Dave/Emma/Ross alibis resolve. R4 = accusations, vote, Buck produces
**Gwen's pigment kit**, full reveal + red-herring wrap.

## The finish (Round 4)
1. Go around the table: each player **accuses** someone and gives one reason.
2. **Vote** (hands or secret ballot); tally aloud. The vote is for fun — the solution is fixed.
3. **Buck produces the kit**; **walk the 5-clue chain** (in the R4 narrator script) so the
   answer is clearly **Gwen**.
4. **Gwen confesses** (campy villain monologue encouraged).
5. **Resolve every red herring** using the wrap so each innocent learns why they looked guilty.
6. Optional **awards** handed out by the Dolan-ghost.

## Troubleshooting
- **A player is absent:** Easiest cuts are Ross (fold his skimming into Emma's story) or the
  Contessa (Antoninski's bribe can go unmentioned). **Never cut Gwen, Buck, or Birdy** —
  they're load-bearing (killer, clue-engine, eyewitness). Reassign a missing key role to a
  confident player or to the host.
- **The table stalls:** Use the narrator host-notes nudges, push players to use their "If
  pressed" lines, or have the Dolan-ghost drift and groan toward the relevant thread.
- **Someone guesses Gwen early:** That's fine — they still need the evidence to *prove* it.
  Withhold the kit (R4) and let the chain play out; reward the deduction at the reveal.
- **A clue didn't surface:** Because clues are redundant (e.g., alibi broken by both Birdy
  *and* Dave; weapon shown by both the coroner's note *and* Buck), the case still closes. At
  the reveal, simply fill any gap from the R4 clue-chain.
- **Keep the weapon facts crisp:** the poison is **Paris Green (arsenical paint pigment)** —
  not moonshine, not a lab toxin. That single fact clears Laurel and points at the art forger.

Have a blast. *My peaches — what a sight.*

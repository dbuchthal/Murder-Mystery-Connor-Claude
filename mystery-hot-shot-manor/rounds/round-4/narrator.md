# Round 4 — Narrator
### "Accusation & Reveal"

## Read aloud (Part 1 — open the floor)

*(The storm's at its worst. Thunder like a kettledrum. The lights flicker — keep a flashlight
handy, it's a nice touch.)*

Well, hot shots, the night's almost spent and the body's gettin' no warmer. We've got a dead
crab-doctor, a glass full of emerald poison, a forger's ledger, a peach glove with somebody's
initials on it, and an eyewitness who saw a certain someone slinkin' outta the study at the
witchin' hour. The bridge is still down. The cops still aren't comin'. Which means the only
justice Dr. Dolan's gonna get is the kind *you* hand down, right here, right now.

So here's how it goes. Everybody who's got an accusation — make it. Lay out who you think
iced the doctor and why. Then we **vote.** Point your fingers, party people. Make 'em count.

*(Run the accusations and the vote — see host notes. Then read Part 2.)*

## Read aloud (Part 2 — the reveal)

*(After the vote. Slow. Let it land.)*

You want to know who did it? Your humble valet had one card left to play. While you were all
busy pointin' fingers, I took the liberty of searchin' the guests' luggage. And in one
particular suitcase — tucked under the silk and the Southern charm — I found a little
traveling case. An **art-restoration kit.** Brushes, solvents, and a jar of emerald powder.

**Paris Green.** A perfect match for what was swimmin' in the doctor's last glass of milk.

The kit belongs to the lady who swore she'd never touched a paintbrush in her life. The lady
whose peach glove turned up at the scene. The lady an eyewitness saw fleein' the study at
eleven o'clock, while she *claimed* to be tucked up in bed. The lady the doctor's ledger was
about to name as the **forger** bleedin' his collection dry.

*(turn to her)*

**Gwen Buchthal. The Georgia Peach.** You forged his paintings, you sold the originals, and
when the old man caught you and threatened to spill it to the world, you stirred your own
emerald poison into his nightcap and left him to die over his puzzle box. *My peaches —
what a sight.*

## The deduction (read or paraphrase — the clue-chain)
1. **The poison was an artist's pigment** — Paris Green — not booze, not a lab chemical.
   That points at someone who works with *paint.*
2. **The forger.** Dolan's will and ledger proved a guest was forging and selling his art —
   the unique motive, and forgers work with pigments.
3. **The glove & the green smudge.** A peach glove monogrammed "G.B." and a pigment smear tied
   the *scene* to Gwen.
4. **The broken alibi.** Gwen swore she was in bed, but Birdy *saw* her leaving the study at
   11:00, and Dave's phone call meant no one could vouch for her.
5. **The kit.** Gwen's own restoration kit held the exact Paris Green that killed him.
   Means, motive, opportunity — all hers, and hers alone.

## Red-herring resolutions (read the wrap so everyone learns why they were suspicious)
- **Dr. Antoninski** *looked* guiltiest — Dolan was about to expose his **faked sasquatch.**
  But he was in the woods with Laurel staging a sighting (10:15–11:30). His secret was fraud,
  not murder — and he needed Dolan discredited, not dead.
- **Laurel** built the fake sasquatch and her moonshine *could* have been the poison — but the
  weapon was paint, not booze, and she was with Antoninski all night.
- **Dave** was desperate for the land deal and shares the initials "G.B." (Buchthal) — but he
  was on the Atlanta phone line 10:30–11:20, and Dolan's death *ruined* his deal. His secret:
  cooking Coca-Cola's books.
- **Birdy** got Dolan's grant money and has the IRS on her tail — but his death triggers the
  audit that sinks her; she needed him alive. Her secret: smuggling and skimming. She's also
  the hero eyewitness.
- **Emma & Ross** alibi each other (her room, all night). Emma's secret: she's broke and came
  to beg a loan. Ross's: he's been skimming from Emma and shopping for a new patron.
- **Christopher Walken** secretly pulled his investment and hid his real name (Chappman) — but
  he performed in the parlor all evening before witnesses.
- **Contessa Savoca** took a bribe from Antoninski and is secretly bankrupt — a scandal, but
  not a murder; she had no window and no weapon.
- **Buck** brought the nightcap (clean!) and was your detective all along.

## Host notes (do not read aloud)
- **Running the accusations & vote:** Go around the table; each player names a suspect and
  gives one reason. Then vote (show of hands or secret ballot). Tally aloud. The vote is for
  drama and bragging rights — the reveal is fixed: **Gwen** is the killer regardless of the
  vote.
- **The kit is Clue 5** — the final, decisive evidence. Present it as a prop (a small case
  with a jar labeled "Paris Green") if you can. Buck delivers it.
- **Gwen's player:** see her R4 handout — she should resist, then crack. Let her have a
  villain's monologue if she wants it ("If it hadn't been for that meddlin' boat woman...").
- **If players accused the wrong person:** walk the clue-chain above so everyone sees how the
  five clues converge uniquely on Gwen, and use the red-herring wrap so each "wrong" suspect
  learns why they looked guilty and why they weren't.
- **Awards (optional, fun):** Best Alibi, Best Accent, Most Tragic Secret, and "The Crab-
  People Would Be Proud." Let the Dolan-ghost hand them out.
- **Timing:** ~30–40 min including accusations, vote, reveal, and wrap.

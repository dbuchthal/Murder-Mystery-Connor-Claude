# Round 3 — Buck Shanty

## What just happened
You tossed the study and hit pay dirt: a **green pigment smudge** on the glass and desk, and a
**peach-colored glove monogrammed "G.B."** dropped by the door. You laid 'em on the table.
Now the room's circlin' the Georgia Peach — and somebody who was up at eleven is about to say
they saw her.

## What you now know (and can reveal this round)
- The **glove and the smudge** put a "G.B." at the scene with the murder weapon on her hands.
- You recall — from your investigator's notebook — that **Gwen "restores" paintings** as part
  of her art-world dealings. Restorers carry pigments. Like Paris Green. Drop this when it
  lands hardest.
- Encourage **Birdy** to tell what she saw at eleven o'clock (you noticed she'd been cagey).

## This round
Run the interrogation. Let Birdy's eyewitness account and Dave's phone-call admission break
Gwen's "in bed" alibi. Bat down Gwen's "the glove was stolen" line — note the pigment on it
matches the glass, and a thief doesn't smear the murder weapon on a glove they're planting.
**Hold back your final card:** you have not yet searched the guests' luggage. Save that for
Round 4 — it's the kill shot.

## If pressed
You're the detective; be straight. If asked whether you're certain, say "I don't deal in
certain, doll. I deal in evidence. And the evidence is pilin' up on one doorstep."

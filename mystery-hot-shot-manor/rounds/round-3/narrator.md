# Round 3 — Narrator
### "The Study Gives Up Its Secrets"

## Read aloud

*(Rain's comin' down hard now, drummin' on the gables like a thousand impatient fingers. The
record skipped and nobody fixed it.)*

All right, hot shots. We know it's murder. We know it's *green.* We know half this house keeps
company with one green poison or another — mushrooms, moonshine, things off a boat. What we
*don't* have yet is the thing every good case turns on: we ain't searched the scene proper. So
I unlocked the study and went over it with the fine-tooth comb the Baltimore PD never paid me
enough for.

Two things, sweethearts. Two beautiful, ugly little things.

**One.** On the desk, and smeared on the rim of that buttermilk glass — a faint streak of
**green.** Same color that stopped the doctor's clock. Whoever doctored that drink left a
little of it behind, smeared like a careless thumbprint. I still can't tell you exactly *what*
it is — but I can tell you the killer carried it in on their hands.

**Two.** *(produce the glove — or describe it)* Tucked half under the study door, dropped in a
hurry — a lady's glove. **Peach-colored.** Soft kid leather, the expensive kind. And stitched
into the cuff, in gold thread, two little initials.

*(beat)*

**"G. B."**

Now. I'm just a valet with a notebook and a bad attitude. But when a peach-colored glove
monogrammed *G.B.* turns up at a murder scene smeared with green — somebody's got some
explainin' to do. And I seem to recall somebody at this table swearin' they were tucked up in
bed all night.

Funny thing about this house. Somebody *else* was up and about at eleven o'clock — and they saw
who was *really* in that study wing. Maybe it's time they spoke up. The floor's open, party
people. Start namin' names — and start answerin' for yourselves.

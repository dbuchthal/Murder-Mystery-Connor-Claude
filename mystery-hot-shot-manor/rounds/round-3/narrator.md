# Round 3 — Narrator
### "Everybody's a Faker"

## Read aloud

*(Rain's comin' down hard now, drummin' on the gables like a thousand impatient fingers. The
record skipped and nobody fixed it.)*

All right, hot shots. We know it's murder. We know it's *green.* And we know — God help us — that
every last one of you keeps a poison in your bag. So I did what any washed-up Baltimore gumshoe
would do: I unlocked the study and went over the scene with a fine-tooth comb.

And you want to know what I found? *Almost nothin'.* A faint smear of green on the rim of the
glass — the poison, and nothin' but the poison. No dropped handkerchief. No monogrammed calling
card. No convenient little clue with a name on it. Whoever did this was careful, or lucky, or
both. The scene points at *everybody* and *nobody.*

*(beat)*

But here's the other thing the old man left behind — his **ledger.** And the ledger says the
forger he caught wasn't faking *receipts* or *résumés.* This one **repainted his canvases** —
swapped his masterpieces for painted copies so good he didn't notice for two years. A forger of
*paintings.*

Now ain't that somethin'. Because the more I talk to you fine people, the more I learn that this
whole room is lousy with fakers. Faked discoveries. Faked provenance. Faked books. Faked names.
Faked heirlooms. Stolen art movin' through the night. You're not a dinner party — you're a
*counterfeit convention.*

So let's hear it. Every one of you has somethin' you've faked, and somebody at this table knows
what it is. Out with it. And while you're at it — somebody explain to me how a man with a case
full of poison mushrooms keeps a straight face right now.

## Host notes (do not read aloud)

- **No glove. No naming eyewitness.** The study search yields only a **faint green residue** on
  the glass — the poison trace, pointing at no one. (This is intentional; the killer is found
  *only* by the art × poison fusion in R4.)
- **Clue 1 sharpened:** the ledger specifies the forgery was **painting forgery** (repainted,
  swapped canvases). **Walken** corroborates: a Dolan canvas was **painted over.** *Hold the
  fusion* — the poison's identity as paint is still unknown until R4, so this just adds "a
  *painting* forger exists" to a room full of other kinds of fakers.
- **GOAL OF THE ROUND: out EVERY guest as some kind of faker, and clear NO ONE.** Every guest's
  fraud angle is questioned and admitted (questioners per bible §7c):
  | Guest | Admits |
  |---|---|
  | Kristof & Laurel | **faked the sasquatch — doctored film, photos, plaster casts** |
  | Ross | **art-fraud cons** — forged provenance & certificates |
  | Emma | **fences forged paintings** through art-world contacts |
  | Walken | **fake identity** (Chappman), passes fakes, murky art "investment" |

    truth stays hidden till R4) |
  | Buck | **faked references/documents** on the job — and reveals he's the **investigator** |
- **REQUIRED: Kristof is hard-pressed as the poisoner this round.** **Birdy** and **Walken** push
  him on his **poison mushrooms** + his blazing motive (Dolan was exposing his fraud). His only
  alibi is Laurel — a fellow proven liar — so it convinces no one. Let him sweat; do **not** let
  him be cleanly cleared.
- **All alibis collapse:** woods (two known fakers vouching for each other), Emma/Ross (a wasted
  woman and a con man), Dave (phone, no witness), Walken (slipped out of the parlor), Birdy
  (alone at the docks, *and* in the house at 11), Buck (handled the glass), Gwen ("in bed,"
  unverified). **Nobody is cleared.**
- **Birdy's "figure in the dark":** she may report seeing *someone* leave the study wing at 11 but
  **cannot identify them** — this proves a sneak was about, implicating no one in particular.
- **END STATE (critical):** by the close of R3 there should be a viable case against *every* guest
  — a poison, a fraud, a motive, and no alibi for anyone. **No single person is the obvious
  killer.** Resist the urge to let the table "solve" it now.
- **How to nudge:** point questioners at targets; keep reminding the room "everyone here has faked
  something and everyone has a poison." If they fixate on one suspect, surface another's fraud.
- **Mechanics & timing:** ~30–35 min. Prop: a vial/swab of green residue (unnamed).

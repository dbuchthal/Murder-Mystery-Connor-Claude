# Round 3 — Narrator
### "The Study Gives Up Its Secrets"

## Read aloud

*(Rain's comin' down hard now, drummin' on the gables like a thousand impatient fingers. The
record skipped and nobody fixed it.)*

All right, hot shots. We know it's murder. We know it's paint. We know there's a forger
slinkin' around this manor with arsenic on their hands. What we *don't* have yet is the thing
every good case turns on: we ain't searched the scene proper. So I unlocked the study. And
your humble detective went over it with the fine-tooth comb the Baltimore PD never paid me
enough for.

Two things, sweethearts. Two beautiful, ugly little things.

**One.** On the desk, and smeared on the rim of that buttermilk glass — a faint streak of
**emerald green.** The same Paris Green that stopped the doctor's clock. The killer touched
that glass with the pigment still on their fingers. Careless. They always are.

**Two.** *(produce the glove — or describe it)* Tucked half under the study door, dropped in
a hurry — a lady's glove. **Peach-colored.** Soft kid leather, the expensive kind. And
stitched into the cuff, in gold thread, two little initials.

*(beat)*

**"G. B."**

Now. I'm just a valet with a notebook and a bad attitude. But in my experience, when a
peach-colored glove monogrammed *G.B.* turns up at a murder scene smeared with the murder
weapon — somebody's got some explainin' to do. And I seem to recall somebody at this table
sayin' they were tucked up in bed all night.

Funny thing about this house. Somebody *else* was up and about at eleven o'clock — and they
saw who was *really* in that study wing. Maybe it's time they spoke up. The floor's open,
party people. Start namin' names.

## Host notes (do not read aloud)

- **Clue 2 lands here:** the **green pigment smudge** on the glass/desk + the **peach-colored
  monogrammed glove ("G.B.")** by the study door — physically tying the scene to **Gwen
  Buchthal.**
- **Clue 4 closes here:** **Birdy** can now reveal she returned for her keys at ~11:00 and
  **saw Gwen hurrying out of the study wing** — directly contradicting Gwen's "in bed" alibi.
  Backed by **Dave's** confirmed phone call (he was on the Atlanta line 10:30–11:20 and
  *cannot* vouch that Gwen was upstairs). Two independent sources — redundant by design.
- **Red herrings resolve this round:**
  - **Antoninski + Laurel** reveal the **woods alibi** (10:15–11:30 staging a fake sasquatch
    sighting) — clears them both of murder and exposes the faked-sasquatch fraud. Suspicion
    leaves Antoninski.
  - **Emma + Ross** confirm they were together in Emma's room — clears both; Emma's
    broke/Ross's skimming may surface as their innocent secrets.
  - **Dave's** phone alibi is confirmed — clears him (and tightens the noose on Gwen).
  - **Contessa's** bribe may wobble into view as Antoninski's fraud breaks — her innocent
    secret.
- **Gwen's defense (let her fight):** she'll claim the glove was "stolen" or "planted,"
  insist she was in bed, and try to discredit Birdy. Let her squirm — the kit (R4) will
  finish her.
- **How to nudge if stuck:** "Whose initials are G.B.?" (Gwen *and* Dave share them — let the
  table briefly wonder, then note it's a *lady's* glove). "Who actually *saw* anyone near the
  study? Birdy — you were up for your keys, weren't you?" Have the Dolan-ghost groan at Gwen's
  "in bed" claim.
- **Mechanics & timing:** ~25–30 min. Present the glove and the "green smudge" as props.
  This is the round suspicion should land firmly on Gwen while the herrings fall away.

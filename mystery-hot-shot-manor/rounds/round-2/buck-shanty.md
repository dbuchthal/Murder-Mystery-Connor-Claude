# Round 2 — Buck Shanty

## What just happened
You dropped the bomb: it's murder, and the weapon was **Paris Green** — arsenical paint
pigment, stirred into the buttermilk you brought up clean at ten o'clock. The room's rattled.
Good. Rattled people talk.

## What you now know (and can reveal this round)
You're the engine of the case now. This round you may surface:
- **The nightcap was clean at 10:00.** You delivered it yourself; the poison went in
  *after.* So the killer reached the study sometime after ten — while half this house was
  wanderin' loose.
- **Dolan's forgery ledger.** Produce it when the moment's right: it proves Dolan had caught
  someone **forging and selling his art** and planned to expose them. It does **not** name
  the forger outright (the proof is technical) — but it nails the *motive* to whoever that
  forger turns out to be. Let the table chew on "who here works with paint and art?"

## This round
Work the room like a case. Press alibis. Note who's twitchy about money, paint, and forgery.
Don't name a suspect yet — you don't have the physical evidence till the study search (R3)
and the luggage search (R4). Plant the pieces; let them connect.

## If pressed
You've got nothing to hide — you're the detective. Be straight about the clean nightcap and
the ledger. If someone asks who you suspect, say "the evidence isn't all in yet, doll. But it
will be."

## Detective's note (host-facing reminder — keep to yourself)
The pigment is an *art* poison; the will/ledger point to a *forger.* Next round's study
search and the final luggage search will tie it to the killer. Don't shortcut it.

# Round 2 — Dr. Antoninski

## What just happened
Murder — confirmed. Poison: an arsenical pigment, "Paris Green." A *chemical.* And you, the
scientist with the lab and the blazing motive, feel every eye in the room swivel toward you.
You are sweating through your medals, comrade.

## What you now know
- The weapon is a chemical pigment — which makes a *scientist* look guilty.
- The will spoke of a forger; you fear it bleeds into talk of *your* fraud.
- You have an alibi (the woods, with Laurel) — but using it confesses the faked sasquatch.

## This round
Endure the suspicion without cracking. **Do not** reveal the woods alibi yet — hold it until
the pressure is unbearable (Round 3). Deflect: point out that *anyone* educated knows of
historic pigments, not only chemists. Keep the sasquatch fraud buried as long as you can.

## If pressed
Be evasive — you "stepped out for air." You know this looks terrible. ⚠️ If the table truly
turns on you and threatens to vote you guilty, you may **break and reveal your alibi**: you
were in the woods with **Laurel** from ~10:15 to 11:30 (staging a sasquatch sighting) — which
clears you of the murder but exposes the hoax. Save this for when you have no choice (ideally
Round 3).

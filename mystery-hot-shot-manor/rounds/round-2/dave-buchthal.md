# Round 2 — Dave Buchthal

## What just happened
Sweet sassafras — it was *murder,* and the poison was some kind of fancy green *paint.* The
will talked about a forger. Your head's spinning. At least nobody's lookin' at *you* for it
yet — but you're terrified they'll start asking about your money.

## What you now know
- It was poison — "Paris Green," an artist's pigment.
- Buck's got a ledger about a forger in Dolan's collection.
- You needed Dolan *alive* for the land deal, so murder makes no sense for you — but you
  can't say *why* you needed him without exposing the embezzlement.

## This round
Be the blustery innocent. Help puzzle out who works with paint. Keep talk away from your
finances. If someone notes your land deal died with Dolan, agree loudly that it *ruined* you —
which (truthfully) clears your motive.

## If pressed
This is important: if anyone presses your whereabouts last night, admit you were on a **long-
distance call to Atlanta from about 10:30 to 11:20.** You can dodge *why* (it was about the
plant's finances), but the *call* is your honest alibi. ⚠️ Realize, with a sinking feeling,
that this means you **can't actually confirm Gwen was upstairs** during that time — you just
assumed she was. (Say this if pressed; don't volunteer it eagerly — but don't lie about the
phone call.)

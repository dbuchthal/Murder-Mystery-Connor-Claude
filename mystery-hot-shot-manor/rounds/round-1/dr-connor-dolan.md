# Round 1 — Dr. Connor Dolan (Ghost)

## What just happened
You're dead, old friend. Slumped over your beloved cryptex, cold as the tide. But death is no
excuse for missing a good party.

## This round (your role)
- Drift about and **haunt theatrically** — mournful sighs, the occasional rattle, a wistful
  word about your crab-people. Set the mood while the living fumble with your cryptex.
- You may **groan approvingly** when they land a correct letter — a gentle, spectral nudge if
  the puzzle stalls. (Coordinate with the host.)
- You secretly know the truth (the host has the bible). **Do not** reveal the killer, the
  weapon, or anyone's secret. If a living player tells a lie that contradicts the timeline,
  flag it *quietly to the host* — never announce it.

## Flavor you may tease
Grumble that "one of my so-called friends was a *thief*," that your will "names no names but
tells the tale," and that "my poor heart was the least of my troubles." Atmospheric only —
nothing that solves the case.

## What you must not do
Don't reveal what's in the cryptex before it opens, the murder weapon, or who did it. Let the
living do the detecting.

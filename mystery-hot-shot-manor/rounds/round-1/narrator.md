# Round 1 — Narrator
### "The Gathering & the Cryptex"

## Read aloud

*(Gravel-voiced. A little smoke. Somewhere, a synthesizer drones.)*

It was the kind of night that makes a fella turn up his collar and question his life
choices. Rain hangin' in the air over La Conner like a debt collector who knows where you
live. And up on the hill — **Hot Shot Manor**, all neon-trimmed gables and bad intentions,
where Dr. Connor Dolan threw the swankiest, strangest party on Puget Sound.

The good doctor promised a gala to remember. He promised to "name a thief and a fraud."
He promised to read a brand-new will that would, and I quote, "rearrange the furniture of
several lives." Big talk for a man who'd be dead before the salad course came 'round again.

Because here's the rub, sweethearts: sometime last night, between the buttermilk and the
break of dawn, **Dr. Dolan checked out permanently.** Found him this mornin' — me, Buck
Shanty, your humble valet — slumped over a crossword puzzle on his desk in the study, 
cold as a Yeti's handshake, draped across that ridiculous puzzle-box safe he loved more 
than people. The **cryptex.**

And wouldn't you know it — the heavens opened, the bridge to the mainland went into the
drink, and the phone line died with a sad little click. No cops. No coroner. No checkout.
Just you, this drafty mansion full of beautiful liars, and one very dead crab enthusiast.

*(beat)*

Now here's your problem, party people. Everything you need to know — the new will, the
names, the *motive* — Dolan locked inside that cryptex. Six letter-rings. One word. 

So that's the deal. Mingle. Scheme. Size each other up. And **solve his crossword** — until 
you can spell the word that springs the lock. Crack the cryptex, and we read the dead man's 
will. *Tick tock, hot shots. The night's not gettin' any younger, and neither is the body.*

## Host notes (do not read aloud)

- **This round is the cryptex puzzle.** No murder-weapon clues yet — Round 1 is about
  meeting everyone, establishing that *everybody* wanted something from Dolan, and forcing
  collaboration. Let the table mingle and trade clues.
- **The answer is `CRABBY`** (Dolan's temperament + his beloved crab-people). Each player's
  R1 handout ends with their cryptex fragment:
  - C (pos 1) = **Christopher Walken** · R (pos 2) = **Ross** · A (pos 3) = **Antoninski** ·
    B (pos 4) = **Birdy** · B (pos 5) = **Buck** · Y (pos 6) = **Laurel**
  - Meta-clues: length=6 (**Gwen**), one letter repeats (**Dave**), it means Dolan's
    temperament/his sea-creatures (**Contessa**), it runs C…→…Y (**Emma**).
- **How to nudge if stuck:** have players read their fragments aloud in turn; if they're
  close, let the Dolan-ghost groan approvingly at correct letters. If truly stuck, allow
  guesses on the dial. The puzzle is a warm-up — don't let it stall the night more than
  ~15–20 min.
- **When they solve it:** announce the cryptex clicks open, then read **The Will** below.
- **Mechanics:** ~25–30 min total. Use a real cryptex, a letter-dial padlock, or a 6-slot
  printout; or simply require the spoken word `CRABBY`. Hand out R2 materials only after
  this round closes.

## The Will (read aloud once the cryptex is open)

*(Unfold the document. Clear your throat. Read like it's a ransom note.)*

> **THE LAST WILL & TESTAMENT OF DR. CONNOR DOLAN**
>
> *To my so-called friends — surprise.*
>
> I have learned, these final months, that I am surrounded by takers. One of you, my
> art-loving guests, is a **thief and a forger** — and I have the proof, written in my
> private ledger. I had meant to name you tomorrow, to the press, to the world.
>
> Therefore I revoke the donation promised to the Seattle Art Museum. I revoke the favors
> promised over land and loans and prizes. **The whole of my fortune** shall fund the search
> for my beloved **crab-people** of the San Juans — administered by the one soul here who
> ever did honest work on the water.
>
> The rest of you may fight over the scraps. You always did.
>
> — *C. Dolan*

## Host notes on the will (do not read aloud)
- This plants **Clue 3 (the forgery motive)** without naming Gwen, and casts suspicion
  *wide*: it threatens Gwen (donation revoked, "forger" = her), Dave (land favor revoked),
  Christopher (loan/investment), Antoninski (prize), and seems to *benefit* Birdy (the
  "honest work on the water" line) — making her look like she had a money motive too. Let
  the table argue about who the "thief and forger" is. The honest answer surfaces over R2–R4.
